# Track 2: The Recruit's Testament

*Bruce's first-person account. The lived experience of being inside this story without understanding it.*

**Voice:** Personal, honest, sometimes confused. The voice of someone who was handed puzzle pieces and only now sees the picture.

**Native abstracts:** VIII (Relinquishment — Joy's essay)

**Primary theme resonance:** Duty vs Conscience

---

## Chapter Outline (Draft)

### 2.1 — Alpha Farm
Thanksgiving 2003. A stranger arrives at a commune in rural Oregon, ten days early. Who is this man? First impressions. The recruitment.

### 2.2 — The Mentor
Three years with Healer (2003-2006). What he was like. The contradictions — soldier and scientist, assassin and humanitarian. The German fluency. The security detail that followed him everywhere and that he sometimes ditched.

### 2.3 — The Stories
What Healer told Bruce. Srebrenica — the HALO jump, the ridge, the five days of watching, the nightmares years later. Germany with Mixter. The "ninja missions." The things that only make sense in retrospect.

### 2.4 — The Patrick Ball Moment
The Cult of the Dead Cow. Hacktivismo. "So, Dr. Ball — vaht can you tell me about zees Dead Cow Cult?" Healer's reaction to the Milosevic cross-examination. The cDc connection.

### 2.5 — The Departure
2006. The keys are surrendered. Bruce doesn't know it's happening. Healer disappears.

### 2.6 — The Silence
Years of trying to understand. The slow pattern recognition. What do you do with knowledge you can't prove?

### 2.7 — Wolfram (2012)
The meeting. The NDA offered and refused. The legally decisive moment.

### 2.8 — The Research
Building the case. The Substack. Finding the GCHQ/Cocks precedent. Hasslacher's trajectory. Freedman's 1988 insight. Kauffman's edge of chaos. The pieces falling into place — or being forced into place by a pattern-matching mind.

### 2.9 — The Reconstruction
This book. The sessions with Claude. The three possibilities. The honest admission: I don't know which one is true.
