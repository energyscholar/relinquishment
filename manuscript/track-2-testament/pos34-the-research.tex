\section*{Search Suppression}

\aidraft{Bruce suspected his Substack (postquantum.substack.com) was being de-ranked in search engines --- articles unfindable unless you already had the direct URL\@. Testing from anonymous library terminals confirmed a selective pattern.}

\aidraft{Indexed and findable: ``Magic Numbers are a Joke'' (innocuous article about magic numbers in computer science). Not indexed and invisible: ``Kangaroos'' (Australian ranch, SAS operatives as ranch hands), ``Time of Troubles'' (IRA sniper incident, SAS recruitment), ``Death of Alan Turing'' (Turing to morphogenesis to Kauffman/Wolfram lineage), ``Relinquishment'' (the full central story).}

\aidraft{If this were poor SEO or low traffic, all articles would be equally invisible. Instead, harmless content is findable while sensitive content is suppressed. The mechanism is not a domain block (too visible) or a takedown (confirmatory) --- it is quiet de-indexing. Good tradecraft: deniable, looks algorithmic, hard to prove.}

\aidraft{The implication is circumstantial: you do not spend resources suppressing noise. You suppress signal.}
