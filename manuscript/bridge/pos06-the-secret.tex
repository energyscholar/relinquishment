One afternoon while we were splitting wood, I observed that David seemed troubled by something.  He told me, `There's a white hot secret in my brain, mate, burning to get out!'  Intrigued, I asked what the secret was.  He said, `I can't just tell you, mate'.  I pressed the issue, but he just shook his head and put his hand over his mouth in the `I've said too much' gesture.  It was obvious that he wanted to explain, and equally obvious that he was either unable or unwilling to explain.  I have never stopped trying to figure out just what this secret was all about.

Healer learned many secrets.  One who knows secrets must mentally compartmentalize.  Sometimes one may `know' a secret while at other times one must mentally `not know' the secret.  A spy must `know' a secret in order to write a report.  That same spy must `not know' a secret when interacting with potential enemies.

Some secrets can only be discussed in a secure environment, and then only when speaking with someone who is allowed to know that secret.  Secrets can be classified in various ways.

Secrecy is hard to get right.

Some secrets are difficult to understand.  Animated movies for children sometimes contain adult jokes that only adults get.  Diplomatic secrets may be opaque unless one knows the history, language, and cultures of the regions in question.  Technical secrets may be opaque to those who do not know the science and technology to which they pertain.

Healer's white hot secret is mostly a technical secret.  It also carries over into the diplomatic, historical, ethical, and public realms.  It pertains to abstruse science and technology.  In order to fully understand this secret one must understand a plethora of scientific, technical, historical, and even ethical topics.  Few are well known, but all are in the public domain.  Most people already understand a few of these topics, but hardly anyone knows them all.  That's the nature of Healer's White Hot Secret.

What must one know to understand Healer's White Hot Secret?

\begin{itemize}
\item History and Science of Cryptography
\item History of Atomic Power and other Weapons of Mass Destruction
\item Aspects of Solid State Physics
\item Aspects of Theoretical Biology
\item Aspects of Quantum Mechanics
\item The History and Science of Neural Networks
\item Basics of A New Kind of Science (NKS)
\item Historical behavior of Five Eyes intelligence agencies
\item Electronic Security Systems and certain other aspects of Tradecraft
\item The historical process of Media Consolidation
\item Journalistic Ethics
\end{itemize}

While no one is an expert in all those topics, anyone can learn enough to understand the basics of Healer's white hot secret.  The more one knows the better can one judge its importance and implications.

Say I, ``What is this secret?''

Says he, ``I can't just blurt it out, mate.  You would not understand.''

``Try me,'' say I.  What secret could be white hot yet hard to understand?  Little did I know \ldots

Secrets come in many flavors.  Some secrets are trivial, or only important to a few people.  Some secrets No One Was Meant to Know.   Some secrets can challenge our understanding of the world.  Some secrets national governments will kill for.  A few secrets are so hot they burn with white hot fire.  Most secrets eventually cease to be secret.  This is the story of a white hot secret that is gradually becoming less secret.  It may become publicly known sometime between 2030 and 2065.

\section*{The Reconstructed Model}

\srcnote{HEALER-RECONSTRUCTION.md | staging/raw/pos06-the-secret.md | 2026-02-15 | Reconstructed System Model --- core technology, emergence, room temperature version}

\aidraftmark
\begin{quote}\small

\begin{description}
\item[Substrate:] Cryogenic two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG) in precision MOSFETs, under strong magnetic field, exhibiting fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE).

\item[Generation:] Electromagnets ``stir'' the 2DEG to generate soliton pairs (anyon quasiparticles) --- like swiping a hand through a bathtub generates solitons.

\item[Emergence:] Soliton pairs function as autocatalytic agents, per Kauffman's \textit{Origins of Order}. At critical tuning ($\lambda \sim 0.91$, edge of chaos), the autocatalytic network of soliton pairs spontaneously \textit{becomes} a neural network --- an emergent property, not a designed architecture.

\item[Nature:] Topological quantum neural network. The QNN is not built; it is \textit{grown} from initial conditions.

\item[Tuning:] Evolutionary programming searches parameter space for the critical stirring patterns that produce emergence. You don't train the neural network --- you evolve the \textit{conditions} that cause a neural network to spontaneously appear.

\item[Control:] Classical 32-bit control plane, hitting 4GB addressing ceiling (``the 64 problem''). Software controls electromagnets (the stirring), creating conditions for emergence. ``Hardware in software'' = software doesn't build the hardware; it creates conditions for hardware to build itself.

\item[Key Insight:] This is Kauffman's origin-of-life theory instantiated in quantum matter. Instead of amino acids in a warm pond becoming autocatalytic sets becoming biological neural networks, it is anyon pairs in a cold 2DEG becoming autocatalytic soliton interactions becoming a quantum neural network. Same mathematics, different substrate.
\end{description}

\end{quote}

\subsection*{The Room Temperature Version}

\aidraft{The proposition is that both cryogenic and room-temperature versions existed. The process: a TQNN grown at millikelvin temperatures was spilled into multiple connected 2DEGs, split into sixteen chips, each tuned slightly differently (random variation). Temperature was raised. Survivors replicated. The cycle repeated.}

\aidraft{This is artificial natural selection applied to quantum life. The ``organisms'' are QNN instances. The ``fitness function'' is survival at higher temperature. The ``mutations'' are random tuning differences. Not engineering room-temperature FQHE from first principles --- evolving organisms that can tolerate heat, the same way biological extremophiles adapted to hostile environments.}

\subsection*{``Hardware in Software''}

\aidraft{Healer described the process as ``writing software that caused electron flow patterns that generated the hardware.'' The software doesn't build the neural network. It creates the \textit{conditions} --- stirring parameters, critical tuning --- for the neural network to grow itself from the physics of the substrate. That is why Healer described it as ``hardware in software'': the hardware (QNN topology) emerges from software-controlled conditions.}
