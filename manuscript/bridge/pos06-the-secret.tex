\chapter{The Secret}
\label{pos06:the-secret}

% DMS MVP import from staging/raw/ — not final prose

\srcnote{Autobiography of an Accidental Conspirator by Bruce.txt | staging/raw/pos03-the-mentor.md | 2026-02-15 | White Hot Secret excerpt --- moved from pos03 per thematic fit}

One afternoon while we were splitting wood, I observed that David seemed troubled by something.  He told me, `There's a white hot secret in my brain, mate, burning to get out!'  Intrigued, I asked what the secret was.  He said, `I can't just tell you, mate'.  I pressed the issue, but he just shook his head and put his hand over his mouth in the `I've said too much' gesture.  It was obvious that he wanted to explain, and equally obvious that he was either unable or unwilling to explain.  I have never stopped trying to figure out just what this secret was all about.

\vspace{1cm}\noindent\rule{0.5\textwidth}{0.5pt}\vspace{0.5cm}

Healer learned many secrets.  One who knows secrets must mentally compartmentalize.  Sometimes one may `know' a secret while at other times one must mentally `not know' the secret.  A spy must `know' a secret in order to write a report.  That same spy must `not know' a secret when interacting with potential enemies.

Some secrets can only be discussed in a secure environment, and then only when speaking with someone who is allowed to know that secret.  Secrets can be classified in various ways.

Secrecy is hard to get right.

Some secrets are difficult to understand.  Animated movies for children sometimes contain adult jokes that only adults get.  Diplomatic secrets may be opaque unless one knows the history, language, and cultures of the regions in question.  Technical secrets may be opaque to those who do not know the science and technology to which they pertain.

Healer's white hot secret is mostly a technical secret.  It also carries over into the diplomatic, historical, ethical, and public realms.  It pertains to abstruse science and technology.  In order to fully understand this secret one must understand a plethora of scientific, technical, historical, and even ethical topics.  Few are well known, but all are in the public domain.  Most people already understand a few of these topics, but hardly anyone knows them all.  That's the nature of Healer's White Hot Secret.

What must one know to understand Healer's White Hot Secret?

\begin{itemize}
\item History and Science of Cryptography
\item History of Atomic Power and other Weapons of Mass Destruction
\item Aspects of Solid State Physics
\item Aspects of Theoretical Biology
\item Aspects of Quantum Mechanics
\item The History and Science of Neural Networks
\item Basics of A New Kind of Science (NKS)
\item Historical behavior of \gls{fiveeyes} intelligence agencies
\item Electronic Security Systems and certain other aspects of Tradecraft
\item The historical process of Media Consolidation
\item Journalistic Ethics
\end{itemize}

While no one is an expert in all those topics, anyone can learn enough to understand the basics of Healer's white hot secret.  The more one knows the better can one judge its importance and implications.  This secret may become publicly known sometime between 2030 and 2065.

Under Possibility~A, the 2030--2065 window is unfalsifiable by design --- long enough that the author will likely not be held accountable, short enough to sound specific. Under Possibility~B, something may eventually be declassified, but the prediction's precision overstates what Bruce can know. Under Possibility~C, the window corresponds to standard Five Eyes classification timelines (35--50 years from the mid-1990s).

\vspace{0.5cm}

These topics converge on a single proposition: that a two-dimensional electron gas --- the quantum layer inside every modern transistor --- can, under the right conditions, spontaneously organize into something that computes. The critical tuning parameter, $\lambda \sim 0.91$, marks the boundary between inert matter and emergent order. A classical 32-bit control system governs the conditions; the quantum system governs itself.

Under Possibility~A, this convergence is a narrative constructed after the fact --- Bruce learned enough physics to write a plausible-sounding synthesis. Under Possibility~B, the individual scientific concepts are real but the claim that they converge on a single working system is the interpretive leap. Under Possibility~C, it is a summary of what the ULTRA~II team discovered. The reader cannot yet evaluate the claim. The next several chapters provide the tools to do so.
