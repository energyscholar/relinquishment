\section*{Complex System Biology}

Complex System Biology applies principles of Complex System Theory to Biology.  Complex System Biology seeks insight into the nature and origin of order, life, and intelligence. Some essential terms:

A \textbf{complex system} is a system composed of interconnected parts that as a whole exhibit one or more properties not obvious from the properties of the individual parts.

\textbf{Emergence} is the way in which complex systems and interactions arise out of a multitude of relatively simple interactions.  Sometimes called ``order for free.''  Intelligence is an emergent property of biological neural networks.

The classic example of a complex system is the ant colony. Each ant doesn't know what the others are doing, but they all play specific roles. From these independent, individual ants comes the order of the surviving colony. The nature of an ant colony is not obvious from studying a single ant in isolation.

It is not at all obvious that a certain type of ``poised'' complex system has the emergent property of being a neural network.  Yet this is known to be so, and is a working basis for Complex System Biology.

Biological neural networks actually exist in nature.  Intelligence is an emergent property of biological neural networks.  Complex System Biology suggests how this might have occurred, and also how it might be replicated.  QNN technology came from trying a Complex System Biology approach in a 2DEG environment.

\section*{The Poised Realm}

Kauffman's ``poised realm'' provides the bridge between quantum mechanics and room temperature. Partial quantum coherence has been demonstrated in biological systems: the chlorophyll/FMO complex maintains quantum coherence at room temperature (Fleming et al., \textit{Nature} 2007). The protein scaffold provides a structured environment where thermal noise actually assists quantum transport.

The proposition is not standard FQHE at room temperature --- which would be a physics violation. It is a Kauffman-style poised state: a different physical regime with empirical precedent in biology. If the magnetosphere's field geometry plays an analogous role to protein scaffolding in photosynthesis --- maintaining partial coherence via structured environment plus evolutionary optimization --- this is an engineering accomplishment, not a physics violation.
