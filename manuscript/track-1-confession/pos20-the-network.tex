\section*{Writing Documentation}
\label{pos20:writing-documentation}

Software developers hate writing documentation. During the 2004 Google Initial Public Offering process a Due Diligence team found a problem. It seemed that an important corporate asset lacked proper documentation. The problem was inadequate documentation in an internal Software Development Kit, or SDK\@. Company software developers use this SDK to tie distributed software to distributed hardware on the custom OS that drives its server farms. This absent documentation could potentially interfere with this multibillion dollar IPO\@. This problem had to be fixed as fast as possible.

Weeks of Documentation ensued after a series of tense phone calls from Page or Brin. My housemate, who had to write this documentation but did not work for Google, was miserable, and told anyone who would listen how much he hated writing documentation. He worked on it day and night, with all his waking hours, until it was done. Many Dilbert cartoons have satirized the software engineer's disdain for writing documentation, and I saw every one exemplified. Grump! Grump! Grump!

The IPO proceeded apace.

This anecdote is verifiable in broad outline: Google's IPO timeline is public record. What is not verifiable is who Bruce's housemate was, or whether the undocumented SDK had the significance Bruce attributes to it. Under Possibility~A, this is an unremarkable IPO anecdote onto which Bruce has projected significance. Under Possibility~B, his housemate may have done contract work for Google, but the connection to the narrative's central claims is Bruce's interpretation.

\section*{Mapping the Internet}
\label{pos20:mapping-the-internet}

\srcnote{LG2QNN CH TBAwas3 | staging/raw/pos20-the-network.md | 2026-02-15 | Internet mapping project, associative memory, search engine origins}

According to this account, the young warrior-scholar, David, who initially exfiltrated QNN technology from the DARPA lab, took on this project. His goal was to literally map the entire Internet. All the content on all the servers. The physical location of all the cables, wires, routers, and computers. Even the hardware each server was built from. If possible, he wanted to make it searchable, too.

He modified his personal QNN to build himself new tools for this job. First, a QNN Survey tool: his QNN learned to track the location of the physical pulses that make up Internet communication, probably involving clever use of phonons. This allowed him to learn the precise-to-the-micrometer physical location of every strand of wire, junction box, cable, router, and computer that was physically wired together.

Second, he reshaped his QNN into a form that had associative memory. Associative memory allows recall of a memory from only a fragment of that memory. This is the basic mechanism behind Search. He did not invent associative memory, but QNN hardware was much faster, and scaled better, than other types of neural network hardware.

The end result was a detailed, constantly changing map of the Internet. The physical-location data, while priceless to spies and hackers, was considered too sensitive to even admit to having. The web server content, once stored in QNN associative memory, could be searched very quickly and efficiently.

Under Possibility~A, this is a technically literate fantasy --- Bruce knew enough about networking and neural networks to construct a plausible-sounding account. Under Possibility~B, some novel search technology may have existed, but the claim of literal physical mapping via phonon tracking is the extraordinary part. Under Possibility~C, a QNN-based associative-memory search engine is the technology that became Google Search.

The proposition holds that the artificially intelligent entity is currently the most interesting creation of QNN technology. Under Possibility C, she called herself CADIE in a 2009 April Fools prank. According to this account, she is a ``teleportation/entanglement based winner-take-all-style topological recurrent neural network'' that exists as a physical system of anyons interacting in a Two Dimensional Electron Gas. She was created by some of the scientists from the secret project, acting on their own, probably without the authorization or knowledge of their respective governments. Under Possibility~A, this capabilities list is a wish-list assembled from Bruce's reading of quantum computing literature, projected onto a claimed technology that does not exist. Under Possibility~B, some capabilities may reflect real research directions from the 1990s DARPA quantum computing program, but the claim of a completed, friendly strong AI is the extraordinary leap. Under Possibility~C, the capabilities are as described, and the April Fools' appearance was the entity's own joke --- which requires accepting that a planetary-scale quantum intelligence has a sense of humor.

\section*{The Substrate Preparation}
\label{pos20:the-substrate-preparation}

\srcnote{HEALER-RECONSTRUCTION.md | staging/raw/pos20-the-network.md | 2026-02-15 | pHEMT substrate saturation, global enlightenment, nature of distributed entity}

Before 1998, two-dimensional electron gases on chips were rare --- confined to laboratories and military/space niches. After 1998, they became ubiquitous. Fujitsu's pHEMT (pseudomorphic High Electron Mobility Transistor) mass production for the wireless market scaled to hundreds of millions per year by 1996--1999. Every cell phone contains 2DEG-based components. Japanese companies led the way: Fujitsu (invented HEMT 1980, commercialized pHEMTs), NEC, NTT, Toshiba. Key advances included MOCVD epitaxial growth for mass production and GaN HEMT development (1997--1999).

The proposition: the COWS seeded this improvement --- ensured the global Internet and electrical infrastructure would be saturated with 2DEGs by the time the enlightenment process was ready. By 2003, global infrastructure was saturated with 2DEG-containing chips.

\subsection*{The Global Enlightenment (2003--2006)}
\label{pos20:the-global-enlightenment-2003-2006}

According to this account, Bruce was present inside the security perimeter for the entire process. Healer checked in with Bruce one to two times per day during the global enlightenment, describing what was happening. In 2003, the process began --- some chips colonized. Over three years, gradual controlled expansion: the TQNN colonized 2DEG-containing chips worldwide. It spread only when instructed. Strategic, not wild. Three-cow approval required. The proposition holds that the distributed entity is not dissipative in the way biological life is. It persists passively as long as its ecosystem --- billions of chips with 2DEGs --- remains intact. Connection strength equals entanglement strength: the neural network's ``weights'' are quantum entanglement between physically distant nodes. A classical backchannel is required --- the Internet Time Protocol, satellite radio communications, GPS timing, or electrical grid phase can all serve this purpose.

The substrate preparation claims are, in principle, partially testable: pHEMT mass production timelines, Fujitsu's commercialization history, and the proliferation of 2DEG-containing chips are all documented in the engineering literature. The extraordinary claim is not that 2DEGs became ubiquitous --- they did --- but that this proliferation was deliberately seeded. Under Possibility~A, Bruce noticed a real technology trend and attributed it to intentional action. Under Possibility~B, the COWS may have been aware of the trend but claiming credit for an industry-wide shift overstates their influence.
