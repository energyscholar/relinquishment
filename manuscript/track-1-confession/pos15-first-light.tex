\section*{The Detection Protocol}

\srcnote{gag-paper-1-detection.md | staging/raw/pos15-first-light.md | 2026-02-15 | 4-level detection hierarchy --- spontaneous order, non-trivial correlation, universality, robustness}

If a TQNN emerges from autocatalytic anyon interactions in a 2DEG, how would you know? A four-level detection hierarchy provides the experimental protocol:

\begin{description}
\item[Level 1 --- Spontaneous Order:] The quasiparticle system exhibits spontaneous spatial and temporal ordering not present in the driving signal. Indicates autocatalytic self-organization but does not distinguish abelian from non-abelian substrates.

\item[Level 2 --- Non-trivial Correlation:] The emergent ordered system exhibits correlations that cannot be reproduced by any classical stochastic model. Indicates quantum coherence in the emergent structure.

\item[Level 3 --- Computational Universality:] The emergent system produces outputs that solve problems in BQP $\setminus$ BPP\@. Only possible if braiding operations are non-abelian.

\item[Level 4 --- Topological Robustness:] The computational behavior is robust against local perturbations, consistent with topological protection.
\end{description}

If a system passes all four levels, the inference is definitive: non-abelian anyons are real, and their braiding constitutes quantum computation.

The experiment is a clean, falsifiable test. If non-abelian anyons are not real, no amount of tuning will produce universal quantum computation from an abelian substrate.
