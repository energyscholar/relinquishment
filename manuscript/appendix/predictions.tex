\section{Falsification Criteria}

What would disprove Option C:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Non-abelian anyons proven physically impossible
\item Room-temperature topological quantum coherence proven theoretically impossible
\item Multiple named figures categorically denying under credible conditions
\item Predicted scientific sequence not appearing despite decades of funded effort
\item Quantum computing developing entirely along conventional lines, no anomalies
\item No whistleblower events despite 40+ years of classification
\item Magnetospheric monitoring finding zero anomalous coherent signatures
\item No institutional signals beyond what public QC progress justifies
\end{enumerate}

If a majority hold by 2040, Option C is effectively dead.
