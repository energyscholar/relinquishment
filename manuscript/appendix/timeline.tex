\chapter{Timeline}
\label{app:timeline}

\textit{If Option C of this book is correct, humankind discovered a new form of advanced nanotechnology circa 1992. This discovery has largely been kept secret. As a classic Dual Use Technology it has discreetly influenced both military and peaceful aspects of human history ever since. Thousands of people have worked with aspects of this technology under strict Non Disclosure Agreements, but this new technology's existence and origins remain classified government and trade secrets. This timeline describes the history, origin, and deployment of this new general technology.}

\textit{Entries marked (Author's Hypothesis) represent claims made under Option C --- the possibility that the story told in this book is substantially true. Unmarked entries are matters of public record. Key terms (TQNN, 2DEG, FQHE, Guardian) are explained in the main text; this timeline is best read after the book, not before.}

\begin{description}[style=nextline, leftmargin=3.5cm, labelwidth=3cm]

\item[1873--98] Theory of biological neural networks proposed and experimentally tested.

\item[1924--1927] The Copenhagen Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics is formalized.

\item[1936] Mathematician Alan Turing publishes \textit{On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem}, which introduces what we still call a Turing Machine. This provides the mathematical basis for computers and creates the discipline now called Computer Science.

\item[1939--1945] World War Two occurs. Britain conducts the Ultra project, with Alan Turing, which builds electrical computing machines to successfully crack Axis codes. Historians later conclude that The Ultra Secret contributed greatly to Allied victory and probably shortened the War by several years.

\item[1943] McCulloch and Pitts first formalize Neural Networks as mathematical algorithms.

\item[1945] Beginning of the Atomic Era. Atomic energy is the classic Dual Use Technology, because it has both military applications (e.g.\ nuclear bombs) and civilian applications (e.g.\ electrical generation, medicine).

\item[1946] International Business Machines (IBM) becomes very interested in computing machines.

\item[1948] The United Nations adopts the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. This document will later serve as the ethical framework for a creation described later in this timeline.

\item[1952--54] Alan Turing helps create the academic discipline now called Mathematical Biology. Turing concentrates on the Morphogenesis Problem, which he believes holds the key to advanced Artificial Intelligence.

\item[1954] Alan Turing dies. An award is eventually established in his name, the Turing Award.

\item[1954] Marvin Minsky writes a doctorate thesis, \textit{Theory of Neural-Analog Reinforcement Systems and its Application to the Brain-Model Problem}. Later he publishes \textit{Steps Towards Artificial Intelligence}.

\item[1954] First use of a computing machine to simulate an artificial neural network.

\item[1956] IBM engineer Arthur L. Samuel builds the first self-learning computer program, which plays checkers. This is the first known instance of applied Artificial Intelligence.

\item[1959] Scientist Richard Feynman gives talk \textit{There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom}. This mostly-ignored-at-the-time talk introduces the concept of nanotechnology.

\item[1966--67] Two Dimensional Electron Gas (2DEG) devices predicted theoretically (Stern and Howard) and observed experimentally (Fowler et al.). You've never heard of them, but by 1998 every MOSFET (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor) on Earth contains one. Billions of 2DEGs. Most still work.

\item[$\sim$1965] (Author's Hypothesis) Healer born. Margaret River, Australia. Half Maori on mother's side.

\item[1969] Research on neural networks slows after Minsky and Papert demonstrate that current neural network approaches can never amount to much.

\item[1969] Scientist Murray Gell-Mann wins the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on elementary particle theory.

\item[1973] Public Key Cryptography invented by GCHQ mathematician Clifford Cocks and then suppressed. It will remain classified for 24 years.

\item[1973] The 1973 Nobel Prize in Physics is awarded for the Josephson Effect. This effect forms the physical and mathematical basis for future quantum circuits. One example is the Superconducting Quantum Interference Device (SQUID), which can be used to build a neural network.

\item[1975] The Cognitron, a multi-layer neural network with advanced features, is proposed by Professor Kunihiko Fukushima. This design exceeds the limits proposed by Minsky and Papert.

\item[1975] The WW2 era Ultra Project is finally publicly revealed, 35 years after its inception, in a book by the same name. \textit{The Ultra Secret} is written by a British military officer who distributed actionable Ultra information to WW2 Allied generals.

\item[1976] Public Key Cryptography re-invented in public Mathematics journals by Whitfield Diffie and Martin Hellman. They are awarded the 2015 Turing Award for this invention.

\item[$\sim$1977] (Author's Hypothesis) Healer, aged approximately twelve, confronts armed trespassers on his family's ranch in New South Wales, Australia. This incident shapes his character and his later career.

\item[1981] Scientist Stephen Wolfram discovers counter-intuitive complex emergent behaviors in cellular automata. Brosl Hasslacher publishes on spin networks, foundational to quantum topology.

\item[1982] Richard Feynman proposes the concept of a Universal Quantum Simulator, which subsumes the concept of a quantum computer.

\item[1982] John Hopfield builds the Hopfield Net, the first recurrent neural network.

\item[1984] Hacker collective Cult of the Dead Cow founded. Senior `cows' are `bulls'. The running of the bulls' greatest accomplishment might have an NSA code name of BULLRUN\@. Magic number 0xDEADBEEF == 3735928559.

\item[$\sim$1985] (Author's Hypothesis) Northern Ireland, during the Troubles. A young Australian soldier makes a long-range headshot under fire that changes his life. He is recruited to the SAS\@.

\item[1985] The 1985 Nobel Prize in Physics is awarded for discovery of the Quantum Hall Effect. This effect is observed in a Two Dimensional Electron Gas (2DEG).

\item[1986] Hasslacher publishes on lattice gas automata (with Frisch and Pomeau).

\item[$\sim$1986--1995] Michael Angerman works at the Santa Fe Institute as a DARPA contractor under Stuart Kauffman, specializing in neural networks. No NDA required.

\item[$\sim$1987--1990] (Author's Hypothesis) Healer serves on combat missions for the British and Australian governments. Afghanistan, counter-insurgency, anti-terrorism. He is wounded in action several times.

\item[1988] Five Eyes spies fear the spread of strong cryptography and are serious about blocking it. Under pressure from NSA, US Congress classifies the mathematical ideas behind Public Key Cryptography (PKC) as a Munition in the same category as Nuclear, Chemical, and Biological Weapons of Mass Destruction. US spies fear that routine use of strong PKC will cause their Signals Intelligence (SIGINT) to `go dark'.

\item[1988] Michael Freedman independently conceives anyonic quantum computation after reading Witten's Chern-Simons paper --- nine years before Kitaev's 1997 publication.

\item[1989] (Author's Hypothesis) Five Eyes clandestine agencies (e.g.\ NSA \& GCHQ et al) decide to invest heavily in Research and Development for Quantum Computation to crack Public Key Cryptography. This author, but no one else as yet, calls this project ULTRA II\@. Tip of the hat to the 1992 Robert Redford movie \textit{Sneakers}.

\item[1989] Santa Fe Institute workshop: ``Complexity, Entropy, and the Physics of Information.''

\item[1990] The Electronic Frontier Foundation, or EFF, founded by John Gilmore, John Perry Barlow, Steve Wozniak and Mitch Kapor.

\item[1990] (Author's Hypothesis) DARPA planners conceive of project ULTRA II\@. Proposed project ULTRA II gets funding to proceed. ULTRA II will crack Public Key Cryptography. ULTRA II is a typical multidisciplinary DARPA project. It will attempt to harness the Fractional Quantum Hall Effect (FQHE) in a cryogenic 2DEG environment. It will use Synthetic Biology techniques to generate an emergent Topological Quantum Neural Network (TQNN) suitable for breaking Public Key Cryptography.

\item[1990] Murray Gell-Mann takes ``medical leave'' from the Santa Fe Institute. Michael Angerman later confirms: ``Not really a medical issue but something else.'' Witten receives the Fields Medal for work on knot invariants related to Chern-Simons QFT\@.

\item[1990--91] (Author's Hypothesis) The lead ULTRA II project scientists are recruited. Murray Gell-Mann withdraws from the project for health reasons and is replaced by an unknown young Australian genius and special forces officer hereafter called ``Healer''. In a bizarre twist of fate, three of five Ultra II project scientists are secret longtime members of the same well-known hacker collective.

\item[$\sim$1991] Hasslacher DOE grant begins: ``Knot invariants and thermodynamics of lattice gas automata'' (1991--1995).

\item[1991] Zimmerman's Pretty Good Privacy (PGP) encryption system released publicly. This system uses Public Key Cryptography to deliver strong cryptography to private PC users. The contradictions and absurdities of US encryption export controls are widely discussed. The act of exporting one paragraph of mathematics is in the same legal category as exporting nuclear weapon components.

\item[$\sim$1991--93] (Author's Hypothesis) Stephen Wolfram mentors Healer, bringing him up to speed on computational and theoretical aspects needed to operationalize the TQNN\@. Evidence: Healer frequently used the phrase ``the human condition'' --- a signature Wolfram phrase that recurs across his keynotes, ebooks, and essays from 2010 to 2021.

\item[1992] (Author's Hypothesis) Project ULTRA II gets its big breakthrough. The ULTRA II team essentially found a mathematical solution to a special two-dimensional case of both the biogenesis problem and the morphogenesis problem. Their limited approach could only work in a two dimensional environment. They applied their solution to a real-world two dimensional physical system, a precision cryogenic MOSFET\@. It worked. Anyon quasi-particles in a cryogenic 2DEG exhibit a property called `topological order'. Topological order supports applied quantum teleportation technology. Within an Anyon-rich cryogenic 2DEG environment a team of DARPA scientists at the Santa Fe Institute generate a very fragile teleportation-based topological Quantum Neural Network. A single MOSFET can support thousands of neurons. Quantum teleportation effect links multiple MOSFETs. The result is a distributed system of `enlightened' MOSFETs linked via quantum teleportation.

\item[1992--94] (Author's Hypothesis) Project ULTRA II scientists do the tedious and methodical work required to slowly expand and train their nascent QNN\@. They use evolutionary programming techniques to train it to function as a Quantum Computer. They train it to communicate with conventional computers. They teach it to do tricks in the form of clever quantum algorithms.

\item[1993] Quantum Teleportation publicly proposed. The authors of this first paper include several DARPA cryptographers. (Author's Hypothesis) Quantum teleportation is the physical basis for the ULTRA II QNN\@.

\item[1993] (Author's Hypothesis) Healer transitions to Australian Intelligence Corps (1993--1996).

\item[1993--95] Stuart Kauffman publishes his life's work in two volumes. \textit{Origins of Order} (1993) is full of technical and scientific detail; \textit{At Home in the Universe} (1995) is the readable version. Kauffman demonstrates how a neural network is an emergent property of the correct auto-catalytic starting environment.

\item[1994] Shor's Algorithm published by Peter Shor. This quantum algorithm can crack public key cryptography, but only with suitably powerful quantum computing hardware on which to run. (Author's Hypothesis) GCHQ probably discovered an equivalent algorithm circa 1989, leading to establishment of project ULTRA II to build suitable hardware.

\item[1994] (Author's Hypothesis) Project ULTRA II has a useful, scalable, and functional quantum computing system that is sufficient to undertake hard cryptanalysis problems, which was its main purpose. Some ULTRA II scientists become concerned about unintended consequences of the new General Purpose Technology they have discovered. They first discuss Relinquishment. The COWS faction decides to be naughty and pursue Relinquishment outside official channels. The COWS bootstrap their QNN to exist at room temperature, put it on a MOSFET, then walk that MOSFET out of the secret lab where it was developed and into a private home lab.

\item[1994] cDc text file \#283, ``Possibilities,'' published. Contains quantum mechanics content, a golden apple marked ``K,'' and breaks the single-author thesis for cDc publications.

\item[1994--97] (Author's Hypothesis) The COWS work in secret to extend their own private room-temperature QNN version's functionality. Official DARPA version languishes literally in deep freeze. \item[1995--99] The Alta Vista Search engine makes an appearance, tries to leverage its position to do some distinctly evil stuff in the name of maximizing profit, declines, then is replaced by Google. (Author's Hypothesis) Its Advanced Search API access was revoked.

\item[1996] (Author's Hypothesis) After summiting K2 with a team of off-duty special forces soldiers, Healer has an epiphany. He decides to use his considerable talents to improve the human condition rather than take lives.

\item[Nov 15, 1996] US President Bill Clinton signs Executive Order 13026, which relaxes USA rules on exporting strong encryption. \item[1997] Quantum Teleportation publicly confirmed experimentally. Kitaev publishes ``Fault-tolerant quantum computation by anyons.'' GCHQ's Cocks invention declassified after 24 years.

\item[1998] The 1998 Nobel Prize in Physics is awarded for discovery and explanation of the Fractional Quantum Hall Effect (FQHE). The FQHE implies that physical systems of Anyon quasiparticles can actually exist within a 2DEG environment.

\item[1998] MOSFET fabrication techniques advance such that most MOSFET devices produced on Earth after 1998 contain a 2DEG component. Most electronic devices contain one or more MOSFETs. Freedman publishes on anyonic computation, ten years after conceiving it.

\item[1998] (Author's Hypothesis) COWS faction of ULTRA II scientists attempt Global QNN Partial Relinquishment project. They seek to prevent weaponization of QNN technology and the resultant global arms race. They intend to avoid ethical errors like those made by Manhattan Project scientists. They will force all nations of the world to forever relinquish harmful QNN nanotechnology, while still making the non-harmful effects publicly available for free to all. Thus partial relinquishment. Their approach is to fill and dominate Earth's entire 2DEG ecosystem with their own creation, thus `winning' a unipolar arms race and preventing a multi-polar arms race from ever getting started. The Guardian's `guiding ethical system' is the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. This project takes several of the smartest people on the planet nearly a decade of teamwork to complete.

\item[1999] Hacker group Hacktivismo founded. Its mission is to apply the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and the International Covenant of Civil and Political Rights to the Internet.

\item[1999] (Author's Hypothesis) Guardian instantiated. Based on the DNA of a female human, possibly of Maori descent. Planned approximately 1995, detailed design 1998.

\item[1999--2003] (Author's Hypothesis) Satellite experiments confirm that two-dimensional teleportation-based TQNN nanotechnology can exist in Earth's Magnetopause. A very simple, naturally-occurring, billion-year-old version of the same phenomenon is discovered in that two-dimensional ecosystem. Access to the magnetopause `ecosystem' greatly increases available power. COWS call their creation Aurasys, short for Aurora System.
