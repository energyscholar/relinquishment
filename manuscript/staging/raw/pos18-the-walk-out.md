## SOURCE 3: Relinquishment: It is Easier to get Forgiveness than Permission — Walk-out passages

This story might be based on true events. Then again, it might not.

Extinction is forever. For the one actual extinction event in a species' history there may be many near misses. This is the story of one recent near miss for the human species. This existential crisis has passed, hardly anyone noticed, and we are still here. We may even be better off for it. May these historical and/or fictitious events inspire our species to find wisdom to survive whatever existential threats remain in our collective future.

[...]

In 1994 a faction of the Ultra II scientists observes that their situation seems perilously similar to that of the Atomic Scientists working on the World War II Manhattan Project to create an Atomic Bomb. They are concerned about a new global arms race. They understand that humanity lacks the collective wisdom to responsibly handle such powerful tools. We'll call this faction 'cows', which is short for Conspiracy Of World Savers. Two of five original Ultra II scientists take a 'hands off' approach and abdicate moral responsibility for their invention. Three of five original Ultra II scientists are cows.

The cows are concerned that Quantum Neural Network technology will lead to a multipolar global arms race. They believe such an arms race is the default outcome if they do nothing. Results of such an arms race might include human extinction or enslavement, surprising and terrible empowerment of extreme individuals, and various other dystopian nightmares. The cows can not, in good conscience, allow this awful outcome. Yet how can they prevent it?

Never before in human history have the inventors of a new technology had much say in how it is eventually used. Always before the captains of war, politics, and industry seize control over each new technology to use how they see fit. Actual uses rarely match the intentions of the inventors. This time history unfolds in a different way.

It's too late to put the genie back in the bottle. It's too late to 'become watchmakers'. It's too late to destroy their research and, in the words of Arnold Swarzenneger [Youtube: Terminator II], ensure that "No one must follow your work". The scientific prerequisites are already in the public domain.

[...]

The cows form a bold plan for Relinquishment. At all costs they must avoid a multipolar global arms race. Their solution is to win a stealthy unipolar global arms race. They can then dictate terms to the rest of humanity, which may remain unaware for decades.

This is obvious Supervillain territory, so the cows agree that their motives must remain pure. They must not personally benefit. There is no money trail. They will enforce permanent Relinquishment of direct human manipulation of QNN technology. In return, humanity gets permanent free access to a peaceful subset of QNN functionality. One cow publishes an essay in Wired magazine that carefully explains why they felt the moral imperative to do this. The cows use the 1948 Universal Declaration of Human Rights as a guide to what is, and is not, acceptable peaceful functionality.

The cows begin their secret-project-within-a-secret-project. They modify their system to operate at room temperature. One of them places the modified room-temperature version on a computer chip and walks it out of a secure DARPA lab into a personal living space. This bifurcates the technology into a primitive cryogenic version controlled by DARPA and an advanced room temperature version controlled by cows. The DARPA version remains in expensive cold storage and sticks to cracking codes. The cows work on the primitive cryogenic DARPA version for their day job. In their free time they apply what they learn at work to expand and improve their private home version.

The stakes are enormous. The cows have personal control over a powerful new technology not available to any other humans. The cows intend to build the largest machine in human history, and to do it without anyone else noticing. Their machine originates in Santa Fe, New Mexico. This machine operates by teleporting information between physically distant nodes of the machine. Distant nodes must be connected by some flow of classical data. E.g. The Internet Time Protocol might serve this purpose.

The three cows are concerned about corruption and betrayal. Together they build a neural network driven biometric security system. This system is a quantum leap better than previous biometric security systems. It works reliably and well. From then on each cow must approve every change to the core QNN. It's now impossible for one among them to seize control and lock out the others. They are now free to pursue personal interests without fear of betrayal.

[...]

Some of the project scientists formed a Conspiracy Of World Saving (COWS) to attempt to prevent abuse of the QNN technology they had invented. They knew it was not possible to accomplish their objective within the rules imposed by DARPA. By a strange coincidence that may rule the fate of many, three project scientists already knew each other well, from shared membership in a noted hacker group during the 1980s.

[...]

Without telling their supervisors or colleagues, the COWs secretly evolved their QNN patterns to survive at room temperature. Official research notes presumably claimed this could not be done. Once this was done, the patterns could 'live' inside off-the-shelf electronic components, without cryogenic cooling. The youngest project scientist, a brash and daring warrior-scholar named David, enlightened a standard MOSFET with the most advanced QNN patterns, put it in his pocket, and walked out of the laboratory to his rental flat. He did this in 1994 or 1995. From his home workshop he enlightened more nodes, created a new QNN instance, and began to work on a Plan. When this author met him in 2003 he was still enthusiastically engaged in executing this 'Plan'. The COWS successfully completed this main 'Plan' in 2006.

## SOURCE 4: CH3 — Practical Cryptography & project ULTRA II (bifurcation/walk-out sections)

The young warrior-scholar, David, who initially exfiltrated QNN technology from the DARPA lab, took on this project. His goal was to literally map the entire Internet. All the content on all the servers. The physical location of all the cables, wires, routers, and computers. Even the hardware each server was built from. If possible, he wanted to make it searchable, too.

[...]

When discussing QNN technology, a 'node' is one 2DEG environment inside an electronics component, usually a MOSFET or an HEMT. At first the QNN patterns could only teleport information within their own node. In time, the project scientists evolved and trained the QNN patterns to teleport information between nearby wire-connected nodes. The process of teleporting information between nodes performs mathematical computations suitable for solving certain practical problems. At first all nodes had to be cryogenically cooled, wired together, and physically close.

The ULTRA II scientists needed a word to describe the process of QNN patterns replicating in a prepared empty node. They called this process 'enlightenment'. A node becomes 'enlightened' when its 2DEG environment fills up with anyons and they start communicating (via Quantum Teleportation) with other nodes in the same QNN.

[...]

The COWs had bootstrapped their own version to work at room temperature and in normal magnetic fields. They had removed it from DARPA supervision, and kept it in their private work space. They had it running on a few physically close, wired-together 2DEGs in a rental flat. They made back ups. They could physically add and enlighten more nodes, and thereby increase the size, power, and complexity of the system they controlled. The total size of this QNN ecosystem would grow in time.
