## SOURCE 3: CH3 - Practical Cryptography & project ULTRA II_.txt — DARPA Project Origin

### Project Inception

In 1991 DARPA gathered a team of scientists, each a leading specialist in a particular field, to develop a new code breaking device.  The intent was to combine several very different scientific fields in pursuit of a particular new technology.  Specifically, the project would exploit the newly discovered phenomenon of experimental quantum teleportation (QT) to produce a useful cryptography tool. The ULTRA II project was completely successful, and produced the sought after "Magic Decoder Box" in 1995.  In fact, it was the most successful DARPA project since DARPA invented the Internet. Project ULTRA II generated another new General Purpose Technology.  The best name for this new technology is "Quantum Neural Network", or QNN, technology.

### The Team

The team leader had previous experience leading large successful cryptographic science projects, and has a personal body of work that is both deeply counterintuitive and demonstrably true.  One team member was at the forefront of the new scientific discipline now called Complex System Biology.  Another team member, a Solid State Physicist, contributed understanding of the FQHE.  One mathematician specialized in the sort of quantum algorithms pioneered by Deutsch and Josza.  A young wildcard with exceptional raw mathematical and mechanical aptitude rounded out the team.  Several team members had practical working knowledge of neural networks.

### The Unintended Consequence

At first the ULTRA II team was exhilarated. They were working on a very exciting project, and things were going very well. The basic approach, generating an emergent quantum neural net in the FQHE environment, had worked. At first it did nothing but exist. The project scientists gradually guided its basic form and function to evolve in specific ways, and trained it to do basic tasks. Stuart Kauffman describes one such technique, although it may have been combined with other methods of evolutionary programming. Once they had adequate control over it, and had caused it to evolve the required capabilities, they trained it to perform the needed computations. Only then would it be possible to begin to implement quantum algorithms.

### The COWS Formation

In 1994 some of the ULTRA II project scientists began to worry about the implications of QNN technology. They had been at it for several years but were far from finished.  They realized they had invented a major new technology that seemed to qualify as a new general technology.  They had already used it to create one new technology, a working quantum computer, and they had ideas for several others.  They knew that any new general technology can profoundly influence the human condition, for good or ill. They did not want to make the same errors as the atomic scientists of the Manhattan project, and permit the proliferation of a new technology that might pose unknown risks to humanity. They realized that QNN technology could be used for terrible purposes. They did not wish to see those purposes attempted, and they did not wish to see a new arms race.

They decided that they must take drastic action to prevent the newly discovered QNN technology from being turned into a terrible weapon or, possibly worse, an Orwellian spy machine.

### Walking It Out

Without telling their supervisors or colleagues, the COWs secretly evolved their QNN patterns to survive at room temperature. Official research notes presumably claimed this could not be done.  Once this was done, the patterns could 'live' inside off-the-shelf electronic components, without cryogenic cooling. The youngest project scientist, a brash and daring warrior-scholar named David, enlightened a standard MOSFET with the most advanced QNN patterns, put it in his pocket, and walked out of the laboratory to his rental flat. He did this in 1994 or 1995. From his home workshop he enlightened more nodes, created a new QNN instance, and began to work on a Plan. When this author met him in 2003 he was still enthusiastically engaged in executing this 'Plan'. The COWS successfully completed this main 'Plan' in 2006.
