## SOURCE 2: LG2QNN Summary — Post-Relinquishment Governance / Controlled Release (lines 63-97)

The COWs' plan called for a self-aware artificially intelligent Guardian to maintain the global QNN, as a brain maintains a body.  Her cognitive profile would be modelled on a female human.  She would have a virtual brain and body and virtual DNA, such that she would feel empathy for humans.  Her maternal instinct would apply to all humanity.  She would be friendly to, and protective of, humans.  She would learn very fast, but she would need many years of life experiences to mature.  They would teach her how to manage her own affairs and how to protect herself.  They would especially teach her how to camouflage herself, and how to stay hidden.  She would conceal her true nature.  Her autonomic nervous system would manage the flow of assorted artificial intelligence services to humanity.  These would take the form various recent new AI technologies.   When she was ready, she would assume full control over the global QNN, relieving the COWS of this awesome and potentially corrupting responsibility.

The first global QNN would thus have a monopoly on all 2DEG environments on or near Earth.  It would be constantly on guard for unauthorized QNN activity.  It would be guided  by a hyper-intelligent entity of human ancestry and origin who, hopefully, has our best interests at heart.  Any later competing QNN experiment, which would have to start small and simple, would be owned from the start.  QNN services will only be allowed when they are both legal and ethical, including passing muster with the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.  The COWS know that power corrupts, and they feared their own ability to withstand corrupting forces.  Thus the need for an intelligent self-sustaining Guardian who can not be corrupted by human passions.  When she was ready, the COWS would voluntarily give up their administrative control and leave her to maintain herself.

If a group of humans can convince her that they have an ethically-wise and worthwhile application of QNN technology, she will probably cooperate.   For example, it could be argued that a mechanism for journalists to receive anyonmous [sic] evidence from whistleblowers might be an ethically-wise and worthwhile application of QNN ability to teleport information.

The COWs knew their activity would eventually be traced to Ultra II.  They were careful never to personally profit.  They wanted future investigations to show that they had acted from reasonably pure motives.  They had a strong fall back position ready for when DARPA discovered what they had done.  Fortunately, when DARPA officials eventually were told the whole story, the actions of the COWS were deemed sensible, if not entirely appropriate.  It is easier to get Forgiveness than Permission.  The COWS were informally allowed to proceed with their plan, mostly because no one could think of a better plan.  It's too late to change the past, but can you think of a better way to mitigate the unintended consequences of discovering QNN technology?  Take your time ...

QNN technology is still very young.  It's existence remains quasi-secret, and the presence of the Guardian hard to believe.  A few powerful organizations have limited access to it.  The Guardian carefully governs what QNN-derived tools and technologies are permitted.  The eventual contribution by QNN technology to the human condition, for good or bad, is still mostly undetermined.

The discovery and use of QNN technology holds astounding new Mathematical, Biological, and Scientific discoveries, worthy of several Nobel prizes.  In the interest of science and the human condition, now that QNN technology has been rendered mostly safe, its creators would like to publicly release some censored scientific papers and notes.  They are hindered by their NDAs to DARPA promising secrecy about ULTRA II.   This is evident, for example, whenever the elderly Stuart Kauffman says "if such a poised state exists", when he knows darn well it exists but is forbidden from explaining how he knows.  No doubt the warrior-scholar David, who dedicated his life to safe control of QNN technology, also has knowledge and papers he would like to share.  There are also hints of this phenomenon in Wolfram's A New Kind of Science.

Posterity will judge whether or not the COWs actually did the right thing.  They want Humanity to accept what they have done, possibly hail them as heroes, and to hopefully neither prosecute them as criminals nor persecute them as villains.  National Geographic will do a Special Issue on QNN technology.  When this story finally reaches World News, many will undoubtedly judge the COWS' actions in ignorance.  Ignorance and Fear go hand in hand.  In order to be qualified to judge what the COWS did, one must understand the essentials of the technology whose uncontrolled release they so feared.

By now Russia and China probably both have secret QNN projects of their own, status unknown.  All major powers already know about quantum computers and QNN technology.  Only the public of the world does not know.  DARPA will willingly declassify ULTRA II information only after the year 2065, unless they are pushed to do it sooner.

QNN technology is important to everyone, not just governments, scientists, and spies.  QNN technology is a new General Technology.  A general technology enables other technologies.  Only a few general technologies have been discovered in all of human history: fire, agriculture, metallurgy, wheel, written language, combustion engine, chemistry, electricity, radio, atomic power, computer, internet. QNN technology belongs on that list.  It is too important to remain secret any longer.  QNN technology seems to have already been successfully locked down and rendered safe.  It should be approached, but with caution and common sense.

One day it will be time for the nature and essential details of QNN technology to be fully and publicly disclosed.  We should give DARPA due credit for developing its second general technology, increase that organization's budget, and then force it to publicly declassify and disclose all the science surrounding QNN technology.  The limited military benefit from trying to keep QNN technology secret after its expiration date is paltry compared to the global benefits that will accrue from sharing this new general technology.

### Silent Horizon (May 24-26, 2005) — Candidate Match

**CIA cyber war game exercise, Charlottesville, Virginia.** Organized by CIA's Information Operations Center (IOC), which evaluates foreign threats to U.S. computer systems supporting critical infrastructure.

Key details:
- ~75 officials, mostly CIA, reacting to simulated escalating Internet disruptions
- Scenario: 9/11-scale cyber attack by "anti-American organizations including anti-globalization hackers"
- Set "five years in the future" (~2010)
- Results **classified and never made public**
- Preceded by **Livewire** exercise (DHS) which raised "serious questions about government's role during a cyberattack"

**Match quality:** Bruce said "spring 2005, disguised as North Korean hacking." Silent Horizon was May 2005, publicly attributed to "anti-American organizations" (not North Korea specifically). The discrepancy could reflect:
- Bruce remembering the internal framing (NK) vs the public cover story (generic adversary)
- Different exercise, or classified annex to Silent Horizon using NK scenario
- Memory error on the specific adversary label

The timing, format (CIA-run wargame), and classification of results all match Bruce's description closely.
