### Complex System Biology and Emergence

Complex System Biology (CSB) applies principles of Complex System Theory to Biology.   Complex System Biology seeks insight into the nature and origin of order, life, and intelligence. Some essential terms in Complex System theory are:

Complex System - A complex system is a system composed of interconnected parts that as a whole exhibit one or more properties (behavior among the possible properties) not obvious from the properties of the individual parts.

Emergence - The way in which complex systems and interactions arise out of a multitude of relatively simple interactions.  Sometimes called 'order for free'.  A particular complex system is said to have a particular emergent property.  For example, intelligence is an emergent property of biological neural networks.

The classic example of a complex system is the ant colony. Each ant doesn't know what the others are doing, but they all play specific roles. From these independent, individual ants comes the order of the surviving colony. The nature of an ant colony is not obvious from studying a single ant in isolation.  An ordered, living ant colony is an emergent property of the behavior and biology of a group of ants.

Emergent properties are, by their nature and definition, not obvious.  For example, it is not at all obvious that a certain type of 'poised' complex system has the emergent property of being a neural network.  Yet this is known to be so, and is a working basis for Complex System Biology.  [reference: Origins of Order, Stuart Kauffman]

Biological neural networks are closely associated with intelligence.  Biological neural networks actually exist in nature.  Intelligence is an emergent property of biological neural networks.  It seems logical that they came into being somehow.   Complex System Biology suggests how this might have occurred, and also how it might be replicated.  QNN technology came from trying a Complex System Biology approach in a 2DEG environment.

## SOURCE 5: Chapter - At the Confluence of the Sciences.txt — Complex System Biology Overview

### Relevant Sections

Complex System Biology

Order for Free, Ordered Regime, Chaotic Regime, GRC, fitness regimes, ecology

A complex system is a damped, driven system whose total energy exceeds the threshold for it to perform according to classical mechanics but does not reach the threshold for the system to exhibit properties according to chaos theory.  Complex System Biology (CSB) studies biology from a complex systems perspective.

Classical mechanics is concerned with the set of physical laws describing the motion of bodies under the action of a system of forces. Classical systems exist in an ordered state, where future behavior is completely predictable.  CSB calls this the 'ordered regime'.

Chaos theory studies the behavior of dynamical systems that are highly sensitive to initial conditions — an effect which is popularly referred to as the butterfly effect. Chaotic systems exist in a state where future behavior is unpredictable.  CSB calls this the 'chaotic regime'.

A complex system exists between the extremes of the ordered regime and the chaotic regime.  It is neither completely predictable, nor completely unpredictable.

CSB applies the tools of complex system analysis to Biology.  For example, a real-world ecology is a complex system.
