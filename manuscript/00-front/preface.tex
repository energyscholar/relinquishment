\chapter*{Preface}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Preface}

This book tells a story I pieced together from three years of conversations with a man who never told me anything classified. He talked about open-source science --- published papers, public lectures, Nobel prizes. I made the connections myself. I have spent twenty years trying to determine whether those connections are real --- whether the story they imply is fabrication, exaggeration, or entirely true. This book transmits that dissonance to you, along with every piece of evidence I have, and the tools to resolve it yourself.

\vspace{1cm}
\noindent\rule{0.3\textwidth}{0.5pt}
\vspace{0.5cm}

\textit{I would tell this story anonymously and for nothing if I could, but that is not possible. The story requires the witness.}

\vspace{0.5cm}

I use real names because anonymized claims cannot be checked --- or at least I don't think I am writing a novel. Under Possibility~A, the names attach to public-domain facts only. Under B or C, they may attach to more --- and the people involved deserve to be identifiable so their contributions can be verified, disputed, or denied.

I do not pretend that the conversations I have recorded can be regarded as verbatim reports. I have a good memory for what concerns me, but these conversations are twenty years old. When the text says ``Healer said X,'' it means my best recollection of approximately what was communicated. The intelligent reader will see where I have reconstructed dialogue and is at perfect liberty to reject it.
