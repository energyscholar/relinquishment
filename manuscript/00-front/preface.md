# Preface

This book tells a story I was told by a man I knew for three years. I have spent twenty years trying to determine whether that story is true.

I cannot tell you which of the following is correct:

**A.** I met a charismatic liar who mixed real science and real names with fiction, and I spent two decades building an increasingly coherent framework around his stories. The framework's consistency is a product of my intelligence, not of underlying truth.

**B.** I met a real person with real connections to classified programs, but the story grew over time — through his retelling and my pattern-matching — from something modest into something extraordinary. The kernel is true. The superstructure is elaboration.

**C.** Everything I was told is substantially correct, and the reason it sounds like science fiction is that it IS the thing science fiction has been trying to imagine.

I have maintained cognitive dissonance about these three options for more than twenty years. It is now easy for me. This book transmits that dissonance to you, along with every piece of evidence I have, and the tools to resolve it yourself.

The book also makes predictions. If Option C is correct, specific events should occur in specific timeframes. I have documented those predictions with dates. As future events unfold, this book is designed to be the context that makes them comprehensible.

If the predictions fail, you have your answer. If they don't, you have a different one.

---

*I would tell this story anonymously and for nothing if I could, but that is not possible. The story requires the witness.*

*— B.S., February 2026*
