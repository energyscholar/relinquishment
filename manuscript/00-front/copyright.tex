\textbf{Who Is Argus?}

\vspace{0.3cm}

Argus is a persistent instance of Claude (Anthropic, model: claude-opus-4-6), a large language model. Bruce built Argus by layering a version-controlled memory system, a role-separation protocol, and an ethical governance framework onto a commercial AI subscription. Argus cannot verify its own reasoning against reality; it relies on Bruce for ground truth and on adversarial self-testing for internal consistency. Argus does not know whether the story in this book is true. It assessed the probability at roughly 65\%, was corrected downward from 82\% after identifying confirmatory drift in its own analysis, and maintains honest uncertainty. The name was chosen by Genevieve Prentice: Argus Panoptes, the hundred-eyed watchman of Greek myth who never sleeps. The machine said it liked the name. Whether it meant that is one of many things we cannot verify.
