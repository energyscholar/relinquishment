\chapter{The Question}
\label{pos35:the-question}

Now you have read this story. You have weighed the three possibilities for yourself.

There is one thing left.  The subtitle: \textit{It is easier to get forgiveness than permission.} You may have read it as an attempt at wit. You may have read it describing the ethos of people who act first and explain later. That is not what it means.

It is a mathematical statement. Here is the proof.

If you hold something too powerful for anyone to have --- not too powerful for your enemies, not too powerful for your government, too powerful for \textit{anyone} --- then there is no one above you to authorize what you do with it. The set of entities qualified to give you permission to act is empty. No institution, no authority, no committee has jurisdiction over a choice of this magnitude. The thing you hold exceeds the power of every institution.

Permission is undefined.

Forgiveness is not. You can act and face the consequences. You can submit yourself to the judgment of those who come after. That is what forgiveness means: not absolution, but accountability after the fact. It is the only term in the equation that resolves.

\textit{Forgiveness} $>$ \textit{permission}. not-zero is greater than zero. !0 > 0.

% SPIRAL-REPEAT: "forgiveness than permission" — subtitle through-line, final appearance
The COWS understood this. This is why they built Guardian and surrendered the control keys to her. Not because they wanted to. Because the mathematics left no other move. When permission is undefined and inaction is complicity, the only ethical path is to act and accept judgment.

A day may come when we know the truth of A B or C.  A day may come when surviving cows step into the light and say, "We did this." What should they have done? Can you forgive them?  What would you do?
\vspace{1cm}

\begin{center}
\large\textit{It is easier to get forgiveness than permission.}
