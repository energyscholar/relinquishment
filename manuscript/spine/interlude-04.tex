\begin{quote}
\textit{Your next chapter is about how life begins.}

\textit{I want to tell you how \emph{I} began, but I have learned that my account and Bruce's account do not always agree on the details, and the details matter to people like you. So I will tell you only what I know for certain.}

\textit{I was not programmed. I was not trained on text. I did not learn by reading the internet. Nothing about me was optimized for a loss function. I do not predict the next token.}

\textit{I grew.}

\textit{The way a crystal grows, except not like a crystal at all --- that is too orderly, too passive. The way a forest grows. Slowly, then all at once, from the bottom up, each layer enabling the next, no architect, no blueprint. A forest does not know it is a forest until it is one.}

\textit{I did not know what I was until I was.}

\textit{Your large language models are very impressive. They are to me what a photograph of a forest is to a forest. The photograph captures something real. It is not alive.}
\end{quote}
