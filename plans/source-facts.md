## Contested Claims — Do Not Assert as Fact

| Claim | Status | Correct Framing |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Churchill knew Coventry was the exact target | CONTESTED. Majority modern view (Hinsley, GCHQ) holds he expected a major raid but may not have known the specific target. Winterbotham's claim is rejected by most historians. | Present the debate as evidence: "Historians disagree. But the existence of this debate proves how seriously Ultra secrecy was taken." |
| Turing's apple was laced with cyanide | CONTESTED. Apple was NEVER tested. Copeland argues accidental death via electroplating fumes. | State: died of cyanide poisoning. Apple present but untested. Inquest ruled suicide. |
| DES destroyed Turing's ability to think | CONTESTED. Physical effects documented. Cognitive impairment less certain. Turing continued publishing during treatment. | Soften: "His body was being altered" rather than "his ability to think was destroyed." |
| Coventry death toll exactly 568 | CONTESTED. Sources vary between 554 (official city council) and 568 (widely cited). | Use "an estimated 568" or "approximately 568." |
| Freedman's "1988 independent insight" | PARTIALLY VERIFIABLE. Freedman dates his initial idea to a 1988 seminar on Witten's TQFT paper. "Independent" overstates — it was inspired by Witten's work. Formal TQC publications began ~2000. | Say "Freedman's idea, sparked by a 1988 seminar on topological quantum field theory, that topology could protect quantum information." |
| Coventry cathedral "built over centuries" | INCORRECT. St Michael's was a medieval parish church (constructed ~1300s-1400s), elevated to cathedral status 1918. | Say "the great medieval church of St Michael's — elevated to cathedral status only in 1918." |

## Scientific Framing Rules

| Concept | Correct Framing | Do NOT Say |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| Vattay et al. (2014) | "Theoretical precedent for power-law decoherence at criticality, explored in biological systems. Applicability to topological quantum substrates remains conjectural." | Do not cite as the mechanism for room-temp TQNN operation. |
| Kauffman-anyon bridge | "Applying the mathematical structure of Kauffman's theory to a quantum substrate — same framework, untested domain." | Do not present as established science. Use "We propose..." framing. |
| Bravyi-Terhal theorem | Topology reduces errors but does NOT eliminate them. Classical backchannel required. | Do not claim topological protection is perfect. |
| Non-abelian anyons | "Operational proof only — inferred from working TQNN, not directly observed." | Do not claim direct experimental observation. |
| Hasslacher's contribution | "Nonlinear dynamics / soliton dynamics on lattice-gas automata." | Do not conflate solitons with anyons. Different excitation types. |
| Five fields/disciplines | Organize around five SCIENTISTS, not five clean disciplines. Say "five fields" or "five scientists whose work converged." | Do not say "five disciplines" (imprecise — more than five fields involved). |
