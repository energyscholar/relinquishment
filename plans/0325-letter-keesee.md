## Anneal Notes

**Rating: 91%**

**What works:**
- Leads with HER program (SWUG), not "look at my stuff"
- Shows depth: 3 direct links + 3 more mentioned + full set URL
- One-prompt angle present but subordinate to physics content
- One clear ask at the end
- No consulting pitch, no money talk, no white paper, no AI governance
- Live clickable links, no attachments

**What I debated:**
- Credential line (Reed College) — cut it. If she clicks one tutorial, the physics speaks for itself. Credential appeals from strangers backfire.
- NH connection ("I have roots in New Hampshire") — cut it. The letter arrives at Morse Hall; she'll notice the return address is Oregon. The SWUG connection is stronger than geography.
- "Peer-review quality" — softened to "rigorous." True but unverifiable claim from a stranger.
- More tutorial links — stopped at 3. Wall of links = none get clicked. Three is enough to show breadth; the "full set" link catches anyone who wants more.

**Risk:** She may not open mail from a stranger. Mitigated: calligraphy letter arrives first, establishes a real human. Email same day confirms it. Worst case she ignores both — no harm done, contact not burned for a later Merkin intro attempt.
