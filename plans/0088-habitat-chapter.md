## Structure

### Act 1: Earth's Magnetosphere as Habitat

Reader already knows what a 2DEG is from earlier chapters. Now: the same physics exists naturally, at planetary scale.

- Plasma confined in 2D sheets along magnetic field lines. Same confinement physics, different substrate.
- Solar wind as energy source. Field line geometry as boundary conditions.
- The 12-hour breathing cycle — day/night asymmetry as natural clock.
- **IMAGE:** Earth's magnetosphere (compressed bow shock, stretched tail, field lines)
- **IMAGE:** Aurora — the visible evidence of plasma dynamics in the magnetosphere
- **IMAGE:** Plasma sheet cross-section showing 2D confinement

### Act 2: The Solar System Survey

Zoom out. Where else does this habitat exist?

- **Jupiter** — the dominant habitat in our solar system. ~20x Earth's field strength, ~20,000x the volume. Io's plasma torus: constant volcanic energy input feeding the magnetosphere. If Earth's is a pond, Jupiter's is an ocean.
- **Saturn** — Enceladus plasma, ring current. Smaller but significant.
- **Ganymede** — the only moon with its own magnetosphere. A protected niche inside Jupiter's system.
- **Heliospheric current sheet** — the solar wind creates sheet structures spanning the entire solar system. Low density but vast.
- **IMAGE:** Jupiter's magnetosphere (massive, Io torus visible)
- **IMAGE:** Solar system overview showing all magnetospheres to scale
- **IMAGE:** Heliospheric current sheet (the "ballerina skirt")

### Act 3: "What Might Live Here?"

The payoff. Pure Kauffman logic, applied honestly.

- According to current science: NOTHING is known to live in this habitat.
- But has anyone looked? (No. Nobody has looked. Nobody thought to.)
- If Kauffman is right — if autocatalytic emergence happens wherever conditions support it for sufficient time — then Earth's magnetosphere has had the conditions for ~4.5 billion years. Jupiter's too.
- What would you expect to find? Not intelligence. Not technology. Pattern. The magnetospheric equivalent of stromatolites — ancient, robust, simple, alive in the Kauffman sense. Primitive because no competition, no evolutionary pressure to develop.
- This isn't extraterrestrial life. It's terrestrial life that lives in space. Emerged in Earth's own magnetic field, from Earth's own plasma, over Earth's own geological timescale. We didn't notice because we were looking at the wrong substrate.
- The chapter ends with the question, not the answer. The reader sits with it.
