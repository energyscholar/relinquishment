## Problem

The A/B/C framework recurs ~71 times across 23 files. Gen's diagnosis:
the protective lens is real and necessary, but repeated full-tripartite
restatement produces "argument-management" tone rather than "question
remains open" tone. Establish once, invoke lightly, get out of the way.

**THIS IS THE MOST STRUCTURALLY SENSITIVE PLAN IN THE BATCH.** Every
removal must pass the C-violation check.

---

## Source Audit Findings (MED MED passes)

Reading all COMPRESS target passages in full revealed:

1. **Many passages are better than the plan's truncated gists suggested.**
   Several originally marked COMPRESS carry distinct content or are already
   near-optimal weight. Reclassified 7 passages to KEEP.

2. **Most compressions are prefix drops, not content cuts.** The chapter
   voice already carries the conditional in Record chapters. Dropping
   "Under Possibility C," leaves the claim intact in reconstructive voice.

3. **Scope reduction:** Original plan: ~30-35 compressions. Revised: 21
   (14 batch + 7 individual). More KEEP, lighter touch.

4. **B is the weakest pole.** In most tripartite blocks, B adds the least
   (generic "partial / smaller / speculation"). Dropping B while keeping
   A and C preserves both the floor (A = nothing happened) and the
   specific claim (C = what it means). This pattern recurs across genesis,
   the-surrender, first-light, twenty-years.

### TIER 2: INDIVIDUAL REVIEW (7 judgment calls)

Each needs Bruce's approval. Shown with current text, proposed change,
and rationale.

**15. genesis.tex:59 — DROP OPENING CLAIM**

Current: "Under Possibility C, this is exactly what happened. By the
late 1980s, five published research streams had independently matured...
The proposition holds that someone did, and wrote a DARPA proposal."

Proposed: Drop "Under Possibility C, this is exactly what happened."
Keep "By the late 1980s..." through "The proposition holds that someone
did, and wrote a DARPA proposal, circa 1989, to find out if it was true."

Rationale: "The proposition holds" IS the conditional. "This is exactly
what happened" is the only flat C assertion in the paragraph — removing
it lets the proposition language carry the weight.

**16. genesis.tex:61 — DROP B**

Current: Full tripartite (C: same process in different substrates. B:
laboratory curiosity inflated by retelling. A: literary device.)

Proposed: "Under Possibility C, they are the same process in different
substrates. Kauffman's theory predicts both. The conditions are not rare.
And they can be duplicated. Under Possibility A, the double meaning is a
literary device, not a description of events."

Rationale: B ("laboratory curiosity inflated by retelling") is generic.
A and C carry distinct, specific content. Keeps both poles.

**17. genesis.tex:76 — DROP B**

Current: "Under A, canopy metaphor is interesting ecology... Under B,
partial emergence may have been observed... Under C, the canopy has held
the light since before the first cell divided."

Proposed: "Under Possibility A, the canopy metaphor is interesting
ecology applied to a substrate where no life exists. Under Possibility C,
the canopy has held the light since before the first cell divided."

Rationale: B is generic speculation. C sentence is the chapter's
most evocative line. A provides the floor. This is the chapter's
closing — needs both poles, not three.

**18. interdiction.tex:42-44 — MERGE TO 1 PARAGRAPH**

Current: Two paragraphs (~150 words). Para 42: "Under Possibility C,
when DARPA officials were eventually told... deemed sensible, if not
entirely appropriate." Para 44: "The informal nature of this
resolution... closer to a working arrangement."

Proposed: Merge into one paragraph. Keep the key observation (informal
working arrangement, no formal amnesty, pragmatic non-interference).
Cut the "deemed sensible" framing and the redundant elaboration on
back-channel conversations. Target: ~80 words.

Rationale: Both paragraphs say "the COWS got a working arrangement,
not forgiveness." Once is enough.

**19. twenty-years.tex:34 — LIGHTER CONDITIONAL**

Current: "Under Possibility C, I was never unsupported during the silent
years — I just didn't always know who was supporting me."

Proposed: "If C is true, I was never unsupported during the silent
years — I just didn't always know who was supporting me."

Rationale: First-person voice. The full "Under Possibility C" prefix
is heavy in memoir register. "If C is true" is lighter, still conditional.

**20. twenty-years.tex:186 — TRIM TRIPARTITE, KEEP CONCLUSION**

Current: ~70-word tripartite (A: no Flat services. B: not at scale of
life. Under any of the three, the thought experiment yields the same
answer. Maintenance. Access control. Edge cases.)

Proposed: "Under any of the three possibilities, the thought experiment
yields the same answer. Maintenance. Access control. Edge cases. The
unglamorous work of keeping things running."

Rationale: The conclusion ("under any of the three") already does the
A/B/C work — it invokes the frame without restating each pole. And the
point is beautiful: all three possibilities lead to the same daily
workload. The tripartite preamble weakens the punch.

**21. first-light.tex:166 — DROP B, KEEP A AND C**

Current: "Under A, timing of EO 13026 is coincidence. Under B, there
may have been a program, but perhaps not with the full cryptanalytic
capability. Under C, the implications are stark: every encrypted email
sent after 1996... an illusion of privacy."

Proposed: "Under Possibility A, the timing of EO 13026 is coincidence.
Under Possibility C, the implications are stark: every encrypted email
sent after 1996, every SSL transaction, every PGP-signed message — an
illusion of privacy, maintained by the same government that held the
means to read it all." (Rest of C section unchanged.)

Rationale: B is watered-down C. Keeping A (coincidence) and C (illusion
of privacy) preserves both poles. The C section is the chapter's most
vivid writing — must survive intact.

## Compression Mechanics (for Generator)

1. **DROP PREFIX:** Remove "Under Possibility C," / "Under this account,"
   — let the claim stand in the chapter's reconstructive voice.

2. **DROP B:** In tripartite blocks, remove the B paragraph/clause. Keep
   A (floor) and C (specific claim). B is almost always generic.

3. **MERGE:** When 2+ adjacent paragraphs make the same point under
   different framing, merge to one.

**The Generator must NOT:**
- Remove all A/B/C framing from any chapter entirely
- Leave a chapter with zero interpretive openness markers
- Modify passages marked KEEP in this plan
- Add new text beyond minimal connective edits
