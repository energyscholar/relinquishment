## The Checklist (Gen's 7-Pass Protocol)

1. **Language sweep:** "demonstrates," "therefore," "it is clear" → uncertainty-aware
2. **Paragraph intent:** convincing vs. showing what it felt like
3. **Structural alignment:** ↑ relinquishment pressure / → neutral / ↓ distracts
4. **Technical material control:** minimal before threshold, allowed after
5. **Witness frame:** not expert persuading but witness reporting
6. **Threshold integrity:** slowed time, no hindsight
7. **Final filter:** "I am not trying to convince you"

**Scope rule:** "The target is not lower confidence. The target
is accurate confidence placement."

| Claim type | Register |
|-----------|----------|
| Established physics | Declarative — stays assertive |
| Informed extrapolation | Bounded language — shows the bridge |
| Speculative synthesis | Conditional language |
| Narrative / reconstructed claims | Full witness frame |

### Why Alpha Farm First

1. **It's the front door to the Record.** The reader's first
   impression of Record-register is set here. If this chapter
   reads as witness testimony, the reader carries that frame
   into every subsequent chapter.

2. **The fixes are surgical.** The bike ride, the Kitchen scene,
   the ground rules — the strongest 80% of the chapter — don't
   need touching. The checklist targets 4 specific paragraphs.
   This lets us test the process on a bounded scope.

3. **The learning is transferable.** The Bletchley comparison
   paragraph is a prototype for courtroom-energy-in-narrative
   that appears throughout the Record. Learning to fix it here
   teaches us how to fix it everywhere.

4. **It's safe.** If the pilot produces a worse chapter, we
   revert. The chapter's best writing (Kitchen, muumuu) is not
   at risk.

### Why The Surrender Last

1. **Needs the threshold interview first.** The "It is done"
   scene is only 2 paragraphs. Gen's 6 interview questions
   are designed to expand it with sensory detail, slowed time,
   and Bruce's emotional state. Without that expansion, removing
   the courtroom energy leaves a hole.

2. **The winning energy is load-bearing.** The "common sense"
   paragraph currently carries the book's central argument.
   Removing it without replacing it with scene/witness isn't
   an edit — it's a structural change.

3. **Highest stakes.** This is the climactic chapter. Getting
   it wrong damages the book more than getting Alpha Farm wrong.

## Anneal

**M1.** The Bletchley comparison serves a real function: it tells
the reader that Alpha Farm's structure (gather unlikely minds in
an isolated place) has historical precedent. Removing too much
loses the framing. Fix: hedge the CLAIM, keep the COMPARISON.

**M2.** The "in my file" paragraph builds the case that Healer's
arrival wasn't coincidence. Under C, this matters. Under A/B,
it's pattern-matching. The scope rule says: narrative claims
get witness frame. The evidence stays. The interpretation hedges.

**L3.** The Assessment section's "speaking plain truth" claim is
the chapter's final word on Healer's credibility. Cutting it
might make the ending feel incomplete. Alternative: end on
"Although my trust was shaken several times, it was never broken"
— which is pure witness (his emotional state, not Healer's truth).
