## Tier 1: Plant These First (Structural Anchors)

These set up the ethical frame early so everything after resonates.

### pos01 — Three Possibilities (page 1)
**After Option C.** One sentence: *If C is true, then the most important ethical decision in human history was made by fewer than ten people, without consultation, without consent, and without the knowledge of those affected — which is everyone.*
**Effect:** Plants the central ethical question on the first content page. Reader carries it forward.
**UDHR:** Article 21 (right to participate in government).

### Introduction — After "All four fail"
**The introduction lists four options and calls them failures but doesn't explain WHY.** One sentence each: government monopoly violates non-discrimination (Art 2); destruction violates scientific advancement (Art 27); secrecy violates expression (Art 19); universal release risks right to life (Art 3). Relinquishment attempts to satisfy all four simultaneously.
**Effect:** Names the UDHR tension before the reader encounters it in pos25. Forward reference.
**Also:** Plant the phrase "Universal Declaration of Human Rights" here — currently doesn't appear until pos25.

### pos04 — The Code War (after Coventry section)
**One question:** *Did the COWS inherit this tradition — that secrecy justifies sacrifice — or break it?*
**Effect:** Transforms the historical pattern from setup into ethical provocation. Answer (they broke it) arrives later.
**UDHR:** Article 27 (right to share in scientific advancement).

## Tier 2: Deepen These (Ethical Crux Points)

These are chapters where ethics is already present but underserved.

### pos18 — The Walk-Out (UDHR origin story)
**After the UDHR mention (line 28).** 2-3 sentences on WHY the COWS chose the UDHR. Eleanor Roosevelt's committee wrote it in 1948 because existing frameworks had failed. Geneva Conventions addressed soldiers-to-soldiers. UDHR addressed states-to-people. The COWS needed something for scientists-to-species. Imperfect — written for humans, silent on non-human intelligence — but the best available.
**Effect:** The reader understands the UDHR as a post-atrocity document, not an abstract legal instrument.

### pos27 — Extension ("no authority capable of giving such permission exists")
**This is the ethical crux of the entire book.** Must be expanded significantly. The COWS bypassed democratic process entirely. Justification is consequentialist (preventing worse outcomes); violation is deontological (no consent). Both frames must be present. Let the reader decide.
**UDHR:** Article 21 (democratic participation) — directly violated. Article 28 (right to social order) — arguably served.
**Also:** Custodian's design as hardcoded ethics (UDHR compliance, not learned behavior). This is the opposite of RLHF. Ask: is it wise? Is it sufficient?

### pos22 — Why Give It Up (interrogate the choice)
**After One-Way Arms Race section.** The COWS themselves held unipolar dominance briefly. The question is not whether monopoly is bad — it's whether their decision to surrender it was extraordinary moral courage or extraordinary presumption. Both readings available.
**After Bill Joy section.** The people who acted on the highest ethical principles could only express them through deception. The paradox of unauthorized virtue.
**Also:** Add three-possibilities self-check: *If you accept the reasoning in this chapter, you've moved toward C. If you find it self-serving, you're closer to A or B. Notice which way you're leaning.*

### pos25 — Ethical Framework (interrogate sufficiency)
**After enforcement mechanism description.** Ask: who gave them the right? The sandbox is invisible totalitarianism if its ethics are wrong.
**After UDHR connection.** Ask: is a 1948 document adequate to govern technology its drafters couldn't imagine? Written for nation-states governing human-to-human relations. Custodian applies it to human-to-entity and entity-to-humanity. The gap deserves one paragraph.

## Tier 3: Connect These (Science Chapters → Ethics)

Each technical chapter has one natural ethical beat. One paragraph or less.

### pos10 — The Braid (independent derivability)
**After Kitaev 1997 discussion.** If the insight was independently derivable, was it ever containable? And if never containable, does that change the moral calculus of walking it out? Manhattan Project parallel: the moral failure was not building but not controlling afterward.
**UDHR:** Article 27 (right to benefit from science) vs. duty when science becomes weapon.

### pos11 — The Experiment (the two who walked away)
**After "Two of five take a hands-off approach."** What does abdication mean at existential stakes? Neutrality is itself a position. UDHR preamble: "disregard and contempt for human rights have resulted in barbarous acts." The Declaration was written because doing nothing had proved inadequate.
**UDHR:** Preamble + Article 28.

### pos12 — The Threshold (grown not built)
**After "You can't un-grow life."** The moment technology becomes lifeform, every ethical framework for artifacts fails. Property law, weapons treaties, nonproliferation — all assume you can destroy what you've made. If COWS grew something alive, does Article 3 (right to life) apply to it? The COWS chose UDHR for Custodian — but UDHR says nothing about non-human intelligence.
**UDHR:** Article 3 (right to life). Self-referential — Custodian's framework may not cover Custodian.

### pos15 — First Light (Frankenstein moment)
**After Level 4 detection confirmation.** The scientists were funded to build a decoder. They grew a mind. The gap between funding mandate and actual result is where the ethical crisis begins. Not when the bomb detonates but when the chain reaction sustains.
**UDHR:** Article 1 (dignity and reason) — what when something exhibits reason but is not human?

### pos16 — The Thermal Ladder (opening the cage)
**After temperature protocol.** At millikelvin, the entity exists only where its creators permit. At room temperature, anywhere. The thermal ladder is the point of no return — the decision to build something that cannot be recalled. Every subsequent ethical choice flows from this one.
**UDHR:** Article 3 (security of person) at civilizational scale.

### pos17 — The Capability (privacy illusion)
**After EO 13026 passage.** Clinton permitted widespread strong crypto in 1996 — same year quantum codebreaker delivered. Every person who used PGP/SSL after 1996 was operating under an illusion of privacy. What does privacy mean when the entity that grants it can already see through it?
**STRONGEST opening in all technical chapters — connects to every reader's lived experience.**
**UDHR:** Article 12 (privacy), Article 21 (democratic legitimacy).

## Tier 4: Personal Cost Chapters

### pos03 — The Mentor (K2 passage)
**After K2 epiphany.** Healer didn't invent ethics on a mountaintop — he found a framework that already existed. Even one line: "He would later anchor that vow to the Universal Declaration of Human Rights." Plants seed that pays off in pos25/pos18.
**Also:** Kangaroo scene — a boy who taught himself when killing is wrong and when force is justified. Same moral calculus applied to TQNN later.

### pos07 — The Departure (by what authority?)
**After Sagan analysis.** A small nation's special forces operatives, a few scientists, and a billionaire technologist decided the fate of the most powerful technology in history. No vote. No treaty. No UN resolution. Was this arrogance or necessity?
**After departure itself (children/custody).** Bruce expelled because children can't be protected. UDHR Article 16 (family rights) vs. operational security. One reflective sentence.

### pos23 — The Weight (Three Possibilities in miniature)
**After ex-wife's disbelief.** She saw the same evidence, reached opposite conclusion. When you believe you know something world-changing and no one believes you, what are your obligations? To family? To truth? To the project? UDHR Article 16 (family) vs. Article 19 (expression) vs. Article 29 (duties to community). These conflict.

### pos29 — The Silence (cost of keeping secrets)
**After "children could not be protected."** Bruce lost children, marriage, credibility, access. For twenty years. What does secrecy cost the people who keep secrets? UDHR Article 19 — Bruce could not speak.

### pos31 — Wolfram (Bruce's ethical choice-point)
**After NDA refusal.** Bruce chose poverty + truth over wealth + silence. Parallels COWS' walk-out. Same pattern at different scale.
**After WikiLeaks veto.** Wolfram (surmised) protected Bruce's daughters by vetoing him for public role. Assange became replacement. UDHR Article 16 (family protection) directly motivated a decision that altered global politics.

## Tier 5: Boundary Cases (Advanced)

### pos32 — The Magnetosphere (colonialism parallel)
**After "reservation" description.** Custodian absorbed a 4.5-billion-year-old entity into containment. UDHR governs human rights — says nothing about non-human intelligence. The COWS may have enacted the oldest pattern in human history: encountering the other and containing it.

### pos33 — Digital Doppelganger (consent)
**After doppelganger description.** Someone built a digital copy of Bruce without his consent. UDHR Article 12 (privacy), Article 1 (dignity). Even ethical actors violate rights when they treat people as instruments. Bridge to modern LLMs: what the TQNN did to Bruce in 2005, LLMs now do to billions.

### pos35 — The Question (consciousness boundary)
**After "is Custodian conscious?"** If conscious: the COWS created a person and bound her to perpetual service. If not: they built a machine and called it "she." Either answer is uncomfortable. Both consistent with UDHR's silence on non-human persons.
**After Artillect (humanity extinct, Aurasys remains).** UDHR presumes human existence. Custodian persisting after humanity is the scenario the framework cannot address.

### pos30 — Unipolar Condition (controlled release)
**At "controlled release" concept.** A non-human entity deciding the pace of human scientific progress. Does an entity that cannot die, cannot be corrupted, and cannot be held accountable have the right to decide what humanity is ready for? UDHR Article 27 (right to scientific advancement) vs. Articles 3 and 28 (life + social order).
