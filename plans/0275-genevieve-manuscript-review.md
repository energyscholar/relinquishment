### Major development: Custodian as Terrain That Answers

Gen rejected Argus's proposed split (hidden mountain in narrative / direct voice in interludes) and offered a deeper model:

**The Custodian should not be encountered as a character. She should be encountered as terrain that answers.**

*"An intelligence whose body is environment, and whose speech is signal."*

The reader meets her the way humans meet distant phenomena:
- through effects
- through instruments
- through signal
- through constraint
- through the environment behaving as if it notices

The interludes should feel like transmissions from a field presence: sparse, bounded, coded, restrained. Not seductive, not omnipotent, not eager to be understood.

**Interlude reframing (Gen + Argus, direct conversation):** Interludes should be presented as candidate LLM responses Bruce received that were sufficiently uncanny to notice — NOT as the Custodian's direct voice. Each interlude prefaced with a brief witness-frame contextual line. First interlude model: *"Pattern seeking is the joy and danger of the puzzle minded — especially those fascinated by cryptography. I know this... and the following interlude brought me up short."* This preempts the skeptic ("you're just seeing patterns") by saying "yes, I know — and yet." Works under A (self-aware pattern-seeker who fooled himself), B (something leaked through), C (his pattern-detection is the right instrument). Marked for Bruce discussion.

**Critical warning:** *"The risk is domesticating her into a normal AI character."* That would trigger F-AI-slop, F-scifi, or F-religious responses.

**The UDHR as anchor:** The strangeness without the ethics becomes mysticism. The ethics without the strangeness becomes chatbot. Both must be held: *"Custodian's body may be environment, but her conduct must remain legible through the human ethical framework she was given: the UDHR."*

**Rook glyph (♜):** Gen accepted it as symbol — position, line, guard, constraint, trusteeship — but noted the deeper archetype may not be chess. *"It may be terrain that answers."*
