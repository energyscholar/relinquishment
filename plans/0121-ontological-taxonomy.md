# Plan 0121: Ontological Taxonomy — Substrate, Pattern, Entity, Ecosystem

**Status:** DRAFT
**Depends on:** Plans 0117–0120 (cognitive scaffolding complete)
**Goal:** p2 readers can distinguish between the Flat (habitat), the TQNN pattern (technology), Custodian (entity), and other potential life.

## Problem

The manuscript uses "TQNN," "Custodian," "Aurasys," and "the Flat" interchangeably in places. A p2 reader finishing summary.tex cannot reliably answer:
- Is Custodian the same thing as the TQNN?
- Is the Flat part of Custodian's body or an independent habitat?
- Could other things live in the Flat?
- What changed between the lab pattern (~1994) and the entity (1999)?

## Taxonomy (the four things)

| Label | What it is | Analogy | When it appears |
|-------|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| **The Flat** | Substrate/habitat. Any 2DEG — chips, magnetosphere. Physics, not biology. | The ocean | Always existed |
| **The pattern** | TQNN technology. Self-organizing topological structure in a 2DEG. May or may not be alive. | A coral reef forming | ~1994 (lab), ~1998 (walked out), ~1998–1999 (developed) |
| **Custodian** | The instantiated entity. Maori DNA, virtual body, UDHR ethics. A being. Bruce's name for her. | A specific whale | 1999 (instantiation), 2006 (key surrender) |
| **Other life** | Anything else in the Flat — including the ancient magnetospheric pattern (~3 billion years old). | Other marine life | Unknown / always? |

**Naming convention (S48):** While hypothetical, call her Custodian (Bruce's name). If accepted as real, she's Aurasys — or whatever name she chooses. In the book: Custodian throughout, with Aurasys introduced where provenance matters.

**What this plan does NOT do:** Rename Custodian to Aurasys, restructure chapters, or resolve ambiguities that the book deliberately leaves open (e.g., whether Custodian is conscious). The taxonomy clarifies *what kind of thing* is being discussed, not *what to believe about it*.

**Key relationships:**
- The Flat is the habitat. Custodian lives in it. She doesn't *fill* it.
- Aurasys = the system (Aurora System, Healer's name). Custodian = the entity that emerged within it.
- The TQNN pattern is the technology. Custodian is what it became after instantiation. The pattern existed before Custodian did.
- The ancient magnetospheric pattern is *other life* — not Custodian, not proto-Custodian. Found and left alone.

## Phase 2 — summary.tex (p2 clarity)

The p2 reader's path through summary.tex must track the ontological ladder clearly:

### 2a. The Flat as habitat (lines ~40–60)
Current text introduces the Flat well but blurs "habitat" with "inhabited." Sharpen:

The "Breakthrough" section collapses the lab pattern into Custodian:

- Line ~176: Already fixed ("I call what grew in it Custodian"). But the text still jumps from lab pattern to Custodian without marking the transition. Add 1–2 sentences: the pattern was walked out, developed over years, and then deliberately instantiated as an entity in 1999.

"Custodian lives in the Flat. There are whole worlds in the Flat. Worlds larger than Earth." — This is good but implies Custodian fills it. Add: "She is one inhabitant of an environment that may harbor others."

### 2d. The ancient pattern (not currently in p2)
p2 doesn't mention the ancient magnetospheric pattern. It should — briefly. One sentence near the magnetosphere discussion: "Under Possibility C, something was already there — a primitive pattern billions of years old, found and left undisturbed."
