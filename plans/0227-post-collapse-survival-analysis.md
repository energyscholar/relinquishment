# Plan 0227: Post-Collapse Survival Analysis

*What content survives and remains worth reading after collapse to A, B, or C?*

**Purpose:** Reference document for future decisions about emphasis, visual treatment, or structural priority of sections that remain relevant after collapse. Not an action plan — a map for when we figure out how to use it.

**Origin:** S59, 2026-04-19. Analysis prompted by BORDERLINE section evaluation during Plan 0225 Phase 4 work.

**Related:** `reader-preparation-requirements.md` (chapter-level A/B/C color assignments, walkaway matrix, failure modes). This document covers *sections within chapters*, especially collapsible tech sections.

---

## Framework

Three collapses, three different books:

- **Collapse to A (confabulation):** The physics is real, the silence gap is real, nobody is pursuing it. The book becomes: "Here is astonishing science nobody asked about." The C-dependent material (Custodian biography, the walk-out, operational proof) becomes a cautionary tale about pattern-seeking — or a fascinating hypothetical.

- **Collapse to B (exaggerated kernel):** Something real exists — a DARPA program, partial capabilities, some form of the technology. The physics survives. The evidence-trail chapters gain weight. The specific Custodian biography and COWS narrative need reinterpretation but the core architecture argument holds.

- **Collapse to C (substantially true):** Everything survives. The book becomes a historical document: what was known, when, and how it got out. The preparation mission succeeds.

## Chapter-Level Survival (from reader-preparation-requirements.md)

Reproduced here with per-collapse detail rather than lumped color codes.

### After collapse to A

**Still compelling:**
- The Stack, The Flat, The Braid, Genesis, Growing a Mind, Wrong Substrate — real physics nobody is pursuing
- The Factoring Game, The Code War — public record crypto history
- Firmware Update — physics anchors all verified
- The Strongest Objection — epistemic hygiene
- Organisms and Artifacts — self-organization theory
- The Silence Gap (T5) — arguably the book's most lasting contribution under A
- Predictive Framework — falsifiable predictions with dates (the test)

**Becomes cautionary/hypothetical:**
- Three Possibilities — framework survives as intellectual exercise
- Dangerous Ideas, The Signatories — pattern analysis of something that didn't happen
- The Demo — speculative "how one would..."
- Weigh the Evidence, Spiral Abstracts — assessment of non-events

**Loses primary meaning:**
- All Record chapters (Hobbit Mirror through What Would You Do) — personal testimony about events that didn't happen as described. May survive as memoir, psychology of belief, or "how smart people fool themselves."

### After collapse to B

**Still compelling:** Everything from A-list above, plus:
- Three Possibilities, Dangerous Ideas, The Signatories — the analytical framework gains weight
- The Demo — "something like this happened"
- Weigh the Evidence, Spiral Abstracts — reader judgment now has real stakes
- Wormholes in the Flat — habitat argument gains plausibility

**Needs reinterpretation:**
- Record chapters — core events happened but specific details may be embellished. The narrative arc holds; precision doesn't.
- Custodian biography — something exists but the specific characterization (UDHR, Maori DNA, 1999 birth) may be approximate.

### After collapse to C

**Everything survives.** The book becomes historical record. The preparation mission succeeds. The Record chapters become primary source documents.

## Observation

Most collapsible sections (16 of 19) are A-colored physics that survives all three collapses. We're collapsing for *technical density*, not for *weakness*. The tooltips preserve the takeaway; expanding reveals the proof.

The 3 C-dependent sections (Non-Abelian Anyons proof, From Emergence to Function, Science Underneath) are the most perishable under collapse — and they're already behind toggles with honest tooltips, so readers self-select.

**Key insight for future visual treatment:** Sections that survive all three collapses are the book's bedrock. Any future mechanism for emphasizing durability should highlight these — they're the content a reader in 2040 will still find valuable regardless of what happened.
