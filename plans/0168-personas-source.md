## Key findings

### The big finding
Three religious personas (Pastor Mike, Amir, Yusuf) collapse T1-T4, T6 to MISSED — not because the prose fails, but because F-religious pattern-matches to theological threat before the physics lands. These readers aren't the majority but they're *loud* — they set discourse in their communities. **The book's UDHR-as-ethics move reads as secular salvation narrative. It is not culturally neutral.**

### Universal weaknesses
- **T8 (guided deduction as deliberate operation)** — 8/9 MISSED-or-PARTIAL. Never surfaces in hook/summary despite book relying on it.
- **T7 (services to tech companies)** — "quietly contracted" fails pragmatic scrutiny (Arjun MISSED) even though other personas flipped to CLEAR in Pass 2. Reads fact-like in what is Possibility-C-conditioned paragraph.

### Structural risks (highest F-mode per persona)
- **Doctorow:** F-conspiracy HIGH — unfalsifiable-by-design architecture is exact conspiracy-theory shape.
- **Arjun:** F-crank HIGH + F-AI-slop HIGH — L2 reader without physics prerequisites + buried LLM disclosure.
- **All 3 religious:** F-religious HIGH.
- **Rachel:** F-scifi HIGH + F-dystopian HIGH — narrative reads as fiction + relinquishment reads as loss.
- **Jane:** F-scifi HIGH — hybrid form blurs physics and narrative.

### Consistency flag (audit methodology)
Pass 2 rated T1/T2/T6 CLEAR across 5 personas. Pass 3 downgraded those cells to PARTIAL/MISSED when religious personas added. Likely both: Pass 2 slightly over-generous, Pass 3 stricter. Religious personas genuinely miss more. Treat Pass 3 as current ground truth.
