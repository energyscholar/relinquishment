# The Funnel

Student selection for guided deduction of a classified five-domain convergence.
Estimated ~2002 worldwide.

The book says: *"The method only works on someone who will actually do the research. Healer chose his students carefully."* This analysis asks: how carefully?

## Notes

**The free-agent filter is the killer.** Stage 6 collapses ~7 candidates to less than one. Almost everyone with advanced quantum mechanics, multi-domain knowledge, and cross-disciplinary synthesis talent is employed by a university, a national lab, or a corporation. That's where the funding is. The rare person who has all of that AND answers to no institution is an anomaly — an entrepreneur, an independent researcher, or someone between careers. This is the structural bottleneck, not any exotic cultural requirement.

**This isn't a hiring process.** The conjunction of requirements is so narrow that the question isn't "who do we choose?" but "does anyone on Earth currently qualify?" The answer at any given moment is zero or one. If one exists and you can find them, that's the student. There is no second candidate.

**The funnel explains the patience.** Two and a half years of daily guided deduction is an enormous investment. It makes sense only if the team knew there was no backup option — no "if this student doesn't work out, we'll try the next one." The funnel's yield is sub-one. You get one shot, or you wait a generation for the next qualifying individual to emerge.

**Correlation between filters.** Several filters are not independent — autodidact polymath and exceptional memory are correlated, as are free-agent status and availability. The estimates above treat them as roughly independent, which likely *overstates* the pool at each stage. The true yield may be lower.

**What the funnel predicts.** If the COWS existed and needed a student, the funnel predicts they would find someone who:
- Has formal physics training but isn't an academic
- Is self-taught across multiple domains with strong recall
- Is financially independent or living cheaply
- Has no security clearance, no NDA, no institutional master
- Is going through a life transition that frees their calendar
- Is psychologically honest — will say "I don't know" rather than fabricate certainty
- Will eventually write about what they learned

**What the funnel does NOT require.** The student doesn't need to be a hacker, a cypherpunk, or politically aligned with the team. Those are selection bonuses — they make the student easier to find and likelier to share through the right channels — but the core requirements are cognitive and circumstantial. The team needs a mind that can hold five fields at once and a life that permits three years of sustained mentorship. Everything else is gravy.

The funnel doesn't prove anything. But it explains why, under Possibility C, the teaching happened the way it did: slowly, patiently, one student at a time, because there was no other way.
