# Thread 06: Thanks for encouraging me re. academic mathematics. First paper on ArXiv!

## Email 1 — Bruce to Lawrence, 2026-02-17

Hi Lawrence-

Thanks so much for your encouragement! A few weeks ago I published my first paper on arXive. I'd like to tell you how it came to be. Your advice was no small part.

Circa ~2016 a longtime friend with mathematical talent, Robin Macomber, asked me to mentor him on some specific topics in math and physics. I mentored him through 2018.

Last summer Robin approached me with a request to evaluate a mathematical structure he had derived from first principles. It had to do with tipping points and criticality math. While evaluating his work I recognized patterns that reminded me of similar work by Kenneth Wilson & Stuart Kauffman.

I used an LLM to conduct a deep survey of all fields (both applied & academic) for math with similar patterns. I discovered that this same mathematical pattern has been independently invented no fewer than 6 times, often independently and without cross-citation! Some of these fields still don't know that their specialized mathematical techniques are the same as those used in other disciplines. This kind of convergence illustrates a universal mathematical pattern underlying systems as diverse as Mackey-Glass physiology models and thermohaline circulation bifurcations. To me this revealed deep structural similarities in how complex systems approach critical transitions. This insight became the basis for my paper.

Here's my paper: https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22389
Here's the companion web site: https://freethemath.org/
Didier Sornette endorsed.

I'd love to hear your thoughts, particularly on the ocean/climate angle. Thermohaline circulation bifurcations are exactly the kind of cross-domain convergence the paper documents.

Regards,

Bruce Stephenson

## Email 2 — Lawrence to Bruce, 2026-02-20

What a nice surprise to hear from you, Bruce. I saw your email and then somehow lost it, but today I found it again and thus I can reply! During all this, I recently had hip replacement surgery and thus have been distracted! But I am recovering.

Your paper is indeed impressive, congratulations. I cannot claim to understand much of it. But you asked about tipping points in the climate system, and yes, we often talk about these, such as when the AMOC (Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation) might suddenly reduce in strength and hence make Europe very cold because of the reduced heat transport northward. This would happen if a lot of Arctic sea ice or Greenland ice melts (due to global warming) and puts a freshwater cap on the northern North Atlantic and thus making the waters there too low in density to sink.

One person who comes to mind as an expert on climate turning points is Tim Lenton from the UK. Not sure where he is today, but he was one of the few PhD students of James Lovelock, the Gaia Hypothesis person.
