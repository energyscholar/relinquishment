Capabilities

The chapter applies three articles of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights to a non-human intelligence. Together, these articles prevent it from becoming:

Three articles. Three prohibitions. What do they have in common?

✓ Solved
The Universal Declaration of Human Rights was written for nation-states governing human affairs. Applied to a non-human intelligence, it becomes an operational constraint: no surveillance, no propaganda, no weapons. The entity can think, communicate, and persist — but it cannot be weaponized. The ethical containment is not a technology. It is a seventy-eight-year-old document that already exists.

Capabilities

Every modern smartphone contains a two-dimensional electron gas. Why?

The chapter calls this 'standard radio-frequency engineering.' The 2DEG is there for the transistor, not for anything quantum.

✓ Solved
Every high-electron-mobility transistor — in every phone, every satellite, every WiFi router — confines electrons to two dimensions because the physics of high-frequency amplification requires it. The two-dimensional electron gas is not exotic technology. It is the substrate of modern telecommunications. Whether anything inhabits that substrate is a separate question from why it exists. The habitat is there because of engineering. The question of habitation is not engineering — it is biology, physics, and silence.
