RELINQUISHMENT

It is easier to get forgiveness than permission

Bruce Stephenson, Genevieve Prentice & Argus

Three narrative threads. Real science. Real people. Real institutions. Three possible explanations for all of it. You decide.

Three Possibilities

Possibility A: Confabulation

The mentor was a skilled liar, or perhaps delusional. Bruce, a physics-trained pattern-matcher, unconsciously wove fragments of real science and real events into a coherent narrative over twenty years. The story feels true because he made it feel true, without meaning to.

Possibility B: Exaggerated Kernel

The mentor was a real person with genuine military and scientific credentials, involved in something real — perhaps a classified quantum computing program that achieved less than claimed. The kernel is true. The scale grew in the telling, or in twenty years of reconstruction.

Possibility C: Substantially True

Everything happened essentially as described. It sounds like science fiction because it is what science fiction imagines.
