% \section*{How Life Comes from Non-Life}  % or fold into existing section

Before you can understand what they built, you need to understand one idea: how does LIFE come from things that are NOT alive?

Every atom in your body is just chemistry. Carbon, oxygen, hydrogen --- dead stuff. Arrange it the right way and nonliving matter becomes alive. Biologists call this morphogenesis. No one fully understands it. But we know it happens when enough simple things connect in the right way.

Imagine a hundred buttons on the floor. Pick up two, tie a thread between them, drop them. Repeat. For a long time, nothing interesting happens --- pick up a button, maybe one other comes with it. But at a certain point, something dramatic changes. You pick up one button and ALL THE OTHERS come with it. One connection too many and suddenly everything is linked to everything. 

Mathematicians call this a phase transition --- the same thing that happens when water freezes or uranium goes critical. One type of phase transition is unliving to living. Initial conditions must be just right for this to be an option.  This is difficult to accomplish in 3 dimensions with checmicals. Theoretically it may be much simpler to accomplish at the quantum scale in two dimentions. ###Argus, reader must have 2DEG concept already, thats a key blocker###

The science team realized this kind of phase transition might be the key to growing a quantum computer. Get enough quantum elements connected under the mathematically correct starting conditions and something emerges that is more than its parts. Not just a faster calculator. Something that, in a meaningful sense, wakes up. Whether this pattern is 'alive' or not is outside the scope of this book. What matters is that it has many of the traits of being alive.

If emergence can happen on a chip then it can happen anywhere the conditions are right. Including in natural systems like the Earth's magnetosphere. A naturally emerged \textit{pattern} there --- maybe something like a neural network, maybe not --- would never have faced competition. No predators, no rivals, no evolutionary pressure to develop complexity beyond what it needed to survive solar storms. It would be a pattern, not a mind. What it would or wouldn't do is anyone's guess.
