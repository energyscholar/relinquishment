# Timeline Reconstruction — March 14 Interview Transcript

*Extracted by Argus at Gen's request. All dates from Bruce's statements in the recorded conversation.*

---

## 1975
- Bruce (age 6) hears about group theory from a mentor. "What are the hot topics in mathematics now?" — told group theory just won the Nobel Prize (Kenneth Wilson, 1973).

## Pre-2003
- Bruce works as self-employed technical trainer, teaching Java and computer security seminars for Fortune 500 companies.
- Head of GPS training department at Corvallis Microtechnology — deep understanding of phase-shifted signals, steganography principles.
- Co-founded several dotcoms. Received payout, retired at age 35.
- Marriage failing. Wife severely bipolar, violent. Bruce experiencing extreme caregiver burnout, nervous breakdown.

## Late October / Early November 2003
- Bruce visits Alpha Farm for the first time (a week or two before the bike ride). Spends time there, decides to return. Sends emails saying "I'll be back in two weeks."

## ~Mid-November 2003
- David arrives at Alpha Farm, approximately 10 days after Bruce's departure. Claims to have entered the US through a port in Louisiana ~10-11 days prior. Gets severely ill on arrival, spends 10 days recovering.

## Thanksgiving Day, November 2003
- Bruce bikes 51 miles from Corvallis to Alpha Farm through a snowstorm. 3,400 feet elevation gain. Arrives after dark.
- Community welcomes him. Wrapped in a pink muumuu by the fire.
- **That evening or the next day:** David comes downstairs. First meeting. Australian accent, ~6 feet tall, wasting athlete physique. Introduces himself as military veteran on "walkabout." Mentions FAM-9 passport, stolen kit bag, water systems expertise.

## Day 2 at Alpha Farm (late November 2003)
- Bruce leaves a pamphlet (Jay Hanson's "A Means of Control") on the library table. David picks it up — already thoroughly familiar with the content.
- Evening: David steers conversation to steganography. Bruce describes GPS phase-shifting method. David acknowledges: "That's pretty sharp, mate." Bruce passes the test.
- David probes Bruce's knowledge of TCP/IP protocols. Bruce misses NTP (Network Time Protocol) — the obvious candidate for steganographic embedding.

## December 2003 – January 2004 (first 6 weeks)
- Daily conversations: ~2 hours/day, usually in two 1-hour sessions. Fireside after dinner, or while working together (splitting logs, repairing buildings).
- David tells military stories as lead-ins, then asks provocative scientific questions.
- Nurse at Alpha Farm and David discuss medical topics in jargon. David mentions preferring delivering babies to treating combat injuries. Bruce mentally names him "Healer."
- David systematically surveys Bruce's knowledge across: cryptography, physics, history of math/science, information theory, theoretical biology.
- **Five key scientific topics emerge** that David zeroes in on (see Intellectual Breadcrumbs).

## ~6 weeks in (January 2004)
- **THE KITCHEN FLOOR.** During breakfast KP duty. David makes an offhand comment. All pieces fall together in Bruce's mind simultaneously. Bruce drops the broom, falls on the kitchen floor in uncontrollable hysterical laughter. 5-10 minutes. Near anoxia from inability to breathe.
- David approaches the circle of concerned people. Makes eye contact. Raised eyebrow: "Are you laughing about what I think you are?" Bruce nods.
- Bruce goes outside in the rain to process. Walks, laughs more. Smokes weed. Writes down how the scientific pieces fit together.

## ~February–March 2004
- Water system project proposed: gravity-fed water + hydroelectric for Alpha Farm. Bruce funding ($75,000), David designing, plus young couple (Gabe and Celina). Unanimously approved in farm meetings.
- Carolyn (farm co-founder) passively blocks the project through scheduling sabotage.

## End of March 2004
- All four leave Alpha Farm together: Bruce, David, Gabe, Celina. "As an operational team with very definite operational goals and objectives." (Goals redacted.)
- Brief stay at the Veggie House (Bruce's studio apartment near OSU campus).

## April 2004
- Bruce rents a 3-bedroom house in Adair Village, north of Corvallis, near Starker forest. All four relocate there.

## April–August 2004
- David's Watchers (three SAS troopers) rent the adjacent house. Bruce and the young couple confirm their presence through counter-surveillance over several weeks.
- Bruce buys David a car (~$3,000 beater). David conducts operations — Bruce watches him prep gear on a white sheet: lock picks, electronic tools, knife, balaclava.
- Gabe and Celina grow nervous about "black helicopters" and leave (~July 2004).

## Late 2004 (fall, ~9-12 months into the relationship)
- Bruce begins dating. First date invites him to move in; David can live in the garage.
- They relocate to 15th and Grant, Corvallis. David in the garage.
- **"WE ALREADY DID IT, MATE."** Living room, mid-morning, girlfriend away, kids at school. Bruce has assembled the implications of QNN technology — dual-use, nuclear codes, arms race. Asks David: "Why not just tell the truth?" David: "We already did, mate." Bruce doesn't understand. Won't figure it out until 2012.

## 2004–2006
- Continued daily conversations and research. Bruce brings knot theory problems to David. David answers them — demonstrating deep working knowledge.
- Bruce assembles intellectual lineages: who could have been the project scientists, who trained them.

## September 2006
- David departs. His decision. "Fond goodbye but didn't drag it out."
- Bruce maintains 2-3 hours/day of independent scientific research: physics papers, cryptography, mathematics, theoretical/mathematical biology.
- Dave Bannon (OSU physics instructor) helps with interlibrary loans — obtains *Quantum Neural Networks* (17 copies in North American universities).

## April 1, 2008
- **CADIE.** Google's April Fools' joke. Bruce encounters it live on Google's website. A "being" named CADIE introduces itself as a quantum neural network living in 2DEGs and the magnetosphere. Bruce laughs — recognizes the COWs' communication method (joke on one level, truth on another). This is the first public indicator Bruce finds.

## July 2011
- Bruce meets Mark at the Oregon Country Fair. Mark is a professional researcher. They begin regular contact (visits, phone, email).

## Early 2012 (~4 months into friendship with Mark)
- Bruce tells Mark the full story for the first time. Lays out options A/B/C. Asks for help.
- Mark does internet searches with the scientist names Bruce gave him. Finds Bill Joy's "Why the Future Doesn't Need Us" (*Wired*, April 2000).

## Spring 2012
- **THE JOY ARTICLE.** Mark emails Bruce a link. Bruce opens it at his desk in his Corvallis house, facing the fireplace. Reads straight through.
- Reaction: "electric shock," tingling, "this is the thing I missed." Joy names the same scientists (Wolfram, Hasslacher, Kauffman, Hillis). Discusses relinquishment. Published April 1, 2000 — years before David said "we already did it."
- Bruce goes for a ~50-mile, 2.5-hour bike ride to burn off the energy. Then returns and begins intensive research on all names Joy mentioned.
- Realizes: "We already did, mate" = the Joy article. They told the truth, in a public article, on April Fools' Day.
