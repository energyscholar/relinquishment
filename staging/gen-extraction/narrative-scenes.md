# Narrative Scenes — March 14 Interview Transcript

*Extracted by Argus at Gen's request. These are moments in the transcript that function as story scenes — spatially grounded, with dialogue, physical detail, and emotional texture — rather than analysis or exposition.*

---

## Scene 1: The Arrival (Thanksgiving 2003)

**Where:** Alpha Farm, Lobster Valley, Oregon. After dark. Snowing.
**Who:** Bruce arriving; Alpha Farm residents inside.

Bruce bikes 51 miles through a snowstorm on a folding Dahon bike. Arrives after dark. His tiny bike light visible through the gloom. Someone opens the door: "It's Bruce. He actually made it." They strip off his wet clothes, wrap him in a blanket by the fire, give him warm drinks. He realizes he's wearing a pink muumuu.

**Strongest detail:** The tiny light seen from inside the house. The community response — several people crowding out, taking his bags, hustling him inside. The muumuu.

---

## Scene 2: David Enters (Thanksgiving evening)

**Where:** Alpha Farm common room. Fire crackling. Bruce on the couch in the muumuu.
**Who:** Bruce, David, others passing through.

Stairs creak. A man comes down. Australian accent. ~6 feet, curly hair, heavy shoulders but wasting — "a super athlete who was atrophying." Military veteran on walkabout. Mentions Louisiana customs, FAM-9 passport, stolen kit bag. First impressions: "immediately could tell from his accent."

**Strongest detail:** The physical contradiction — "buffed-out endurance athlete whose body was eating itself." Gen challenges: "That's a big contradiction." Bruce explains it precisely.

---

## Scene 3: The Nurse and David (Day 2-3)

**Where:** Alpha Farm, communal space.
**Who:** David, the nurse midwife, others.

David and the nurse switch into medical jargon. She's delivered babies and dealt with healthy women. He's dealt with combat injuries but also delivered babies. He wishes he'd done more babies. She says: "I don't wish I'd done any more wounds."

**Strongest detail:** The moment Bruce mentally names him "Healer" — triggered by this exchange. A man of violence who preferred bringing life into the world.

---

## Scene 4: The Steganography Test (Day 2 evening)

**Where:** Bruce's second-floor room at Alpha Farm. David stops by to chat.
**Who:** Bruce, David.

David steers conversation from *The Last Hours of Ancient Sunlight* to steganography. Bruce rattles off a dozen methods. David narrows: "hiding signals in other signals." Bruce draws on his GPS training — describes phase-shifted time jitter as a steganographic channel. David: "Yeah, that's pretty sharp, mate. Now I think that would work."

Bruce realizes he passed a gate. David begins opening up about cryptography.

**Strongest detail:** The evaluative moment — David's acknowledgment is the gate. After this, the conversations deepen.

---

## Scene 5: The Kitchen Floor (January 2004)

**Where:** Alpha Farm kitchen. After breakfast. 8-10 people in the room. Bruce is sweeping. David was washing dishes.
**Who:** Bruce, David, ~8 other residents.

David makes an offhand comment — one last piece in a weeks-long chain of guided deduction. Everything falls into place in Bruce's mind simultaneously. He drops the broom. Falls on the kitchen floor. Hysterical, uncontrollable laughter for 5-10 minutes. People surround him: "Bruce? Are you okay?" He can only gasp "I'm alright!" The nurse pulls out her stethoscope.

David approaches the circle of concerned people. Makes eye contact. Raised eyebrow: *Are you laughing about what I think you are?* Bruce nods. David leaves.

Bruce goes outside alone in the rain. Laughs more. Probably smokes weed. Begins writing down how the pieces fit.

**Strongest detail:** David's raised eyebrow — the entire transaction communicated wordlessly. Also: the anoxia risk from laughing so hard he couldn't breathe.

---

## Scene 6: Watching David Prep

**Where:** Adair Village house, north of Corvallis. David's room, door open.
**Who:** Bruce observing David.

David lays out a white sheet. Places tools on it in exact order: lock picks, electronic tools, knife, black balaclava, pouches. Everything has its place. He packs a rucksack — "optimized for quick access where he knew where everything was."

Bruce: "I was watching a special operations agent work. It was just obvious." Gen: "Is that what you thought at the time?" Bruce: "Yeah... It was like watching the fictitious character Jason Bourne get ready for a mission. Except he had no firearms."

**Strongest detail:** The white sheet. The precision. No firearms.

---

## Scene 7: "We Already Did It, Mate" (Fall 2004)

**Where:** Girlfriend's house, 15th and Grant, Corvallis. Living room. Mid-morning, ~10 AM. Girlfriend away, kids at school. David's garage door open.
**Who:** Bruce, David.

Bruce has spent ~9-12 months assembling the implications. He understands QNN technology is dual-use — nuclear codes, arms races, surveillance, potential extinction. He asks David: "Why not just tell the truth, mate? Put it out somewhere. It won't be believed, but just tell the truth."

David looks at him: "We already did, mate."

Bruce doesn't understand. Asks follow-up questions. David won't elaborate. Bruce goes and plays computer games. Kids come home from school. Normal evening resumes.

**Strongest detail:** The ordinariness after the revelation. Computer games, kids, beer. The meaning invisible for 8 more years.

---

## Scene 8: Opening Mark's Email (Spring 2012)

**Where:** Bruce's house in Corvallis. Sitting at his desk facing the fireplace. Late spring, warm.
**Who:** Bruce alone.

Mark sends an email with a link. Bruce clicks. *Wired* magazine. April 1, 2000. Bill Joy. "Oh — new technologies... ethical implications of dual-use technologies... he discusses relinquishment... he coined the term... Oh my gosh, he names Wolfram, Kauffman, Hasslacher, Danny Hillis."

Bruce: "It was like an electric shock. I started tingling. This is the thing. This is the thing I missed."

Reads to the end. Realizes the Joy article IS what David meant by "we already did it." Goes for a 50-mile, 2.5-hour bike ride to burn off the energy. Returns. Begins intensive research on all names mentioned.

**Strongest detail:** The tingling electric shock. The 50-mile bike ride as a processing mechanism. The 8-year gap between "we already did it" and understanding what it meant.

---

## Scene 9: CADIE (April 1, 2008)

**Where:** Unspecified (Bruce doesn't remember where he was).
**Who:** Bruce alone at a computer.

Bruce encounters Google's April Fools' page. A being called CADIE introduces itself as a quantum neural network living in 2DEGs and the magnetosphere. Names the five scientific topics Bruce had been studying. Bruce laughs: "This is funny. This is exactly in line with how the COWs operate. They say something that's a joke on one level and true on another."

**Strongest detail:** Bruce's laughter — the same response pattern as the kitchen floor. Recognition expressed as humor. Also: this predates the Joy article discovery by 4 years — Bruce saw CADIE first but didn't have the full picture yet.

---

## Scene 10: The Coda — Why Bruce Treasured It

**Where:** The cabin in Alsea, recording winding down. Near midnight.
**Who:** Bruce, Gen.

Bruce reflects on the mentorship. After three days at Alpha Farm he was shocked David was still there. After six weeks, still shocked. He treated every day as a blessing. "I'm getting a better education here than I could get at any university. I could be paying $60,000 a year to go to MIT." Five radically different scientific topics plus tradecraft.

They played chess. Bruce beat David soundly. "He didn't want to play chess after that. He was pretty shocked. A sound drubbing."

Gen: "That was the greatest." Bruce: "I'm glad you thought so."

**Strongest detail:** The chess. David — genius-level hacker, SAS captain, multilingual polymath — beaten at chess by the cyclist in the muumuu. And refusing to play again.
