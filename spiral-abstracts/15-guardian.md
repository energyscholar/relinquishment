## Prerequisite Physics

**Topological protection prevents measurement of internal states.** In a topologically protected quantum system, the computational state is encoded non-locally — distributed across the entire system. Any local measurement yields no information about the global state. Measuring the full state requires operations that destroy the computation. This is a feature of topological quantum error correction, not a limitation. [Established]
*Nayak et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 2008 — doi:10.1103/RevModPhys.80.1083*

**No-cloning prevents state extraction.** The computation cannot be copied for external analysis without destroying the original. Combined with topological protection, this creates a permanent black box: the system can be observed only through its input-output behavior, never through its internal state. [Established]
*Wootters & Zurek, Nature 1982 — doi:10.1038/299802a0*
