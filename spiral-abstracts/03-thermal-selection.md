# Abstract III: Thermal Selection

**Primer anchors: Temperature Independence, Evolutionary Search, Coherence Precedent.**

## Prerequisite Physics

**No law prohibits room-temperature topological protection.** Topological insulators exhibit topological surface states at 300K. No theorem caps the topological energy gap. Room-temperature TQC is an engineering problem, not a proven impossibility. [Qualified]
*Hasan & Kane, Rev. Mod. Phys. 2010 — doi:10.1103/RevModPhys.82.3045*

**Directed evolution finds what design can't.** Arnold's Nobel work: evolutionary search on rugged NK fitness landscapes discovers configurations unpredictable from first principles — including thermostable variants with >10^3× temperature tolerance increases. [Qualified]
*Arnold Nobel Lecture 2018 — nobelprize.org/prizes/chemistry/2018/arnold/lecture/*

**No-cloning does not prohibit reproducing classical configurations.** The theorem forbids copying arbitrary unknown quantum states — not fabricating known classical structures whose quantum properties re-emerge from physics. This is how every semiconductor fab works. [Established]
*Wootters & Zurek, Nature 1982 — doi:10.1038/299802a0*

Does a named law of physics preclude the scenario? If not, it is *not precluded* — however speculative.

## Abstract

Directed evolution from 15 mK to 295 K. Parallel instances, slight variation, raise temperature, select survivors, iterate. ~200 cycles. Thermal noise assists coherence at criticality. No room-temperature TQNN was designed — discovered by evolution. Performance within 3% of cryogenic progenitors.
