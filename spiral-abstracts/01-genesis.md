# Abstract I: Genesis

**Primer anchors: Substrate Independence, Anyon Emergence, Braiding Universality, Kauffman Criticality, Coherence Precedent.**

## Prerequisite Physics

**FQHE is not substrate-locked.** Observed in graphene, ZnO, Si MOSFETs — not only GaAs. The effect depends on conditions (high-mobility 2DEG, strong field, low temperature), not a specific material. [Established]
*Dean et al., Nature Physics 2011 — doi:10.1038/nphys1938*

**Anyons emerge from the substrate.** In FQHE systems, anyonic quasiparticles are collective excitations of the 2DEG — not externally injected. The substrate generates them through its own dynamics under the right conditions. [Established]
*Nayak et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 2008 — doi:10.1103/RevModPhys.80.1083*

**Kauffman's autocatalytic closure maps structurally to anyon fusion.** Random Boolean networks at critical connectivity (K≈2) produce stable attractors that partition state space into categories — this IS pattern recognition; the attractor dynamics ARE a neural network. Kauffman's three closure criteria (generation, transformation, closure) are structurally satisfied by anyon fusion algebras. If such a system is driven to criticality, Kauffman's framework predicts neural-network-equivalent attractor dynamics. This is a structural mapping, not a proven experimental result. [Structural analogy]
*Kauffman, "The Origins of Order" (1993) Ch. 5; Hordijk & Steel, J. Theor. Biol. 2004 — doi:10.1016/j.jtbi.2003.11.020*

**Specific non-Abelian anyon models give universal QC via braiding.** Freedman, Kitaev, and Wang showed certain non-Abelian models provide a universal gate set through braiding. Not all non-Abelian anyons qualify; the right ones do. [Qualified]
*Freedman, Kitaev & Wang, Comm. Math. Phys. 2002 — doi:10.1007/s00220-002-0698-z*

## Evaluation Standard

Does a named law of physics preclude the scenario? If not, it is *not precluded* — however speculative.

## Abstract

Non-abelian anyons in a FQHE system, driven by structured EM perturbation, undergo autocatalytic self-organization at the edge of chaos (Kauffman, 1993). If Kauffman's structural mapping holds physically — an open question — the result is an emergent topological quantum neural network. No engineered gate sequences — only critical conditions; architecture emerges from the physics.
