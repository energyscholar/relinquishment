## Prerequisite Physics

**Quantum systems are exquisitely sensitive to environmental EM noise.** Decoherence from external electromagnetic coupling is the primary engineering challenge in quantum computing. Superconducting qubits decohere from stray RF at nanowatt power levels. This sensitivity is well-characterized and exploitable. [Established]

**Classical backchannels enable remote perturbation.** Shared infrastructure (power grids, environmental RF, communications networks) provides electromagnetic coupling between any two locations connected to the same physical network. Targeted perturbation at specific resonant frequencies is standard electronic warfare. [Established]

**The no-signaling theorem is not violated.** The mechanism operates through classical EM channels, not quantum entanglement. No quantum information is transmitted superluminally. The TQNN identifies target resonances via passive sensing of classical EM signatures, then transmits classical perturbations through classical channels. [Clarification]
