# Abstract VI: Exodus

**Primer anchors: Cloning Boundary, Substrate Independence.**

## Prerequisite Physics

**No-cloning does not prohibit reproducing classical configurations.** The theorem forbids copying arbitrary unknown quantum states — not fabricating a known classical structure whose quantum properties re-emerge from physics. A semiconductor fab does this every day: it fabricates a known classical lattice, and quantum properties (band structure, mobility, tunneling) emerge from the physics, not from copying a quantum state. [Established]
*Wootters & Zurek, Nature 1982 — doi:10.1038/299802a0*

**FQHE is not substrate-locked.** Observed in graphene, ZnO, Si MOSFETs. Conditions matter, not material. If a TQNN configuration is defined by classical structure (dopant arrangement, geometry, interface properties), it can be reproduced in any compatible substrate. [Established]
*Dean et al., Nature Physics 2011 — doi:10.1038/nphys1938*
